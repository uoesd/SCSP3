Waverly Braces for More Rain as Tropical Storm Ida Approaches

As Tropical Storm Ida bears down on Waverly, Tennessee, residents are bracing themselves for the possibility of additional flooding and damage. The town is still reeling from recent flooding that killed 20 people and damaged countless homes, and the thought of more rain is a daunting one.

"It's like reliving a nightmare all over again," said Melissa "Kay" Anderson, a local resident who had to evacuate her home during the recent flooding. "We're still trying to recover from the last flood, and now we have to prepare for another one."

According to John Cohen, a meteorologist with the National Weather Service, Hurricane Ida has been downgraded to a tropical storm, but it is still expected to bring 2 to 5 inches of rain to the area. "Ida will weaken into a depression, but it will still bring significant rain," Cohen said.

Waverly is under a flash flood watch, with continuous rain expected for 24 hours starting late Monday. The town's Emergency Management Agency is working to prevent creek blockage to avoid further flooding. "We're doing everything we can to make sure the creeks don't overflow," said Grey Collier, spokesperson for the Humphreys County Emergency Management Agency.
