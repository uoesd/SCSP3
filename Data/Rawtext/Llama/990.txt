Twitter Bans President Trump, Citing Role in Inciting Violence

In the aftermath of the U.S. Capitol riot, Twitter's senior policy executive, Vijaya Gadde, made an emotional appeal to colleagues regarding the potential ban of President Trump's account. The move came as Twitter employees were divided, concerned for their safety due to security threats and frustrated that Trump's account was reinstated after a 12-hour ban.

"We have to think about the impact that our decisions have on the world," Gadde said in a meeting, according to a person familiar with the matter. "We can't just think about it as a free speech issue. We have to think about it as a safety issue."

Two days later, on January 8, Twitter ultimately banned President Trump's account, citing his role in inciting violence and marking the end of an era of perceived online free speech championed by the platform. The decision followed years of internal debate within Twitter and was marked by tearful meetings and bitter arguments among employees.

"We have to take responsibility for the impact that our platform has on the world," Twitter CEO Jack Dorsey said in a statement. "While we recognize the importance of free expression, we also recognize that our platform has the potential to incite violence and spread harmful ideas."

The move also sparked broader concerns over social media's power and the potential need for regulatory oversight, leading to international dialogue.
