Twitter Suspends Republican Congresswoman Marjorie Taylor Greene's Account Over Election Fraud Claims

Twitter temporarily suspended the account of Republican congresswoman Marjorie Taylor Greene on Thursday, citing violations of its integrity policy. The suspension came after Greene posted baseless claims of election fraud related to the Georgia Senate elections.

In a statement, Twitter said that it had flagged at least two of Greene's tweets as "disputed" and limited their distribution due to the "risk of violence." The company did not specify which tweets had been flagged, but Greene has been vocal in her support for Donald Trump and has embraced QAnon conspiracy theories in the past.

Greene criticized Twitter's actions as censorship and claimed that Americans' rights are being stripped away. "Conservatives are being targeted by the internet police for their political opinions," she said in a statement. "This is a clear violation of my freedom of speech and the freedom of speech of all Americans."

Greene's suspension follows the permanent ban of Donald Trump from Twitter and the removal of over 70,000 accounts with QAnon ties. Tech giants' actions against platforms and individuals with conservative views have sparked outrage among Republicans.

"I'm being silenced for speaking the truth," Greene tweeted before her account was suspended. "The truth is that the 2020 election was stolen and the people responsible are trying to cover it up. #StopTheSteal."

Greene's claims of election fraud have been widely debunked by election officials and fact-checkers. The Georgia Secretary of State's office has repeatedly stated that there is no evidence of widespread fraud in the state's elections.

Despite this, Greene has continued to push conspiracy theories and has been vocal in her support for Trump. In a recent tweet, she called the former president "a hero" and claimed that he was "fighting a global cult of Satan-worshipping paedophiles."
