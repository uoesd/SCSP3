Senate Democrats Push for $2,000 Stimulus Checks as First Order of Business

Senate Democrats, led by Minority Leader Chuck Schumer, are making additional COVID-19 relief a top priority, including $2,000 stimulus checks for eligible Americans. Schumer has committed to delivering on the promise of increasing direct payments to a total of $2,000, a key issue in the recent Georgia run-off elections.

"We're going to make sure that the American people get the relief they need," Schumer said in a statement. "We're going to fight for $2,000 stimulus checks, because the American people deserve it."

The proposal for $2,000 checks has been supported by both President Trump and President-elect Joe Biden. The federal government has previously sent two rounds of stimulus checks, up to $1,200 and $600, to eligible Americans. The new stimulus bill aims to supplement the $600 payments with an additional $1,400, alongside funding for vaccine distribution, small businesses, schools, and state and local governments.

However, not all Democrats are on board with the idea of sending Americans $2,000 stimulus checks. Senator Joe Manchin, a moderate Democrat from West Virginia, has expressed opposition to the proposal.

"I'm not sure that sending another check for $2,000 is the best way to go," Manchin said in an interview. "We need to focus on vaccination, that's the key to getting our economy back on track."

Manchin has questioned the employment benefits of additional checks, suggesting that the funds could be better spent on vaccination efforts.

Despite the opposition, Senate Democrats are confident that they can pass the bill with the support of Vice President-elect Kamala Harris, who will cast the tie-breaking vote in the 50-50 split Senate.

"We're going to work hard to get this bill passed," Schumer said. "We have the support of the American people, and we're confident that we can get it done."

The approval of another massive relief bill is uncertain, however, due to potential lack of support from some Democrats. Senator Manchin's opposition highlights the challenges that Senate Democrats may face in passing the bill.

"It's going to be a tough road, but we're committed to getting this done," Schumer said. "We're going to work with our colleagues on both sides of the aisle to make sure that the American people get the relief they need."

The push for $2,000 stimulus checks is the first order of legislative business for Senate Democrats, and they are determined to make it a priority.

"We're not going to let up until we get this done," Schumer said. "The American people are counting on us, and we're going to deliver."

In conclusion, Senate Democrats are making a push for $2,000 stimulus checks a top priority, with the support of Vice President-elect Kamala Harris. While there may be opposition from some Democrats, Senate Minority Leader Chuck Schumer is committed to delivering on the promise of increasing direct payments to a total of $2,000. The proposal is part of a larger stimulus bill that aims to supplement the $600 payments with an additional $1,400, alongside funding for vaccine distribution, small businesses, schools, and state and local governments.
