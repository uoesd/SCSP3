"Liz Cheney, I don't know what's happened to her," Trump said. "She's not doing well. She's not doing well at all."

Trump's recent appearances and statements have shown his continued influence within the Republican Party, despite his loss in the 2020 presidential election.

"He's still the leader of the Republican Party, whether people like it or not," said Doug Heye, a Republican strategist and North Carolina native.

However, Trump's return to the political stage comes as prosecutors in New York and Georgia have intensified investigations related to him. Trump allies have also been accused of attempting to oust Republicans who have spoken out against him.

"It's clear that Trump and his allies are trying to purge the party of anyone who dares to speak out against him," said Ammar Moussa, a Democratic Party spokesperson. "It's a dangerous and divisive strategy that will only harm the Republican Party in the long run."

Despite being banned from major social media platforms, Trump hinted at resuming mass political rallies soon.

"We're going to start having rallies again, and they're going to be bigger and better than ever before," Trump said. "We're going to make America great again, and we're going to make America strong again."

Trump's speech was met with enthusiasm from the crowd, who chanted "USA! USA!" and "Trump! Trump!" throughout his address.

"He's still got it," said one attendee, who asked not to be named. "He's still the man who's going to make America great again."

However, not everyone was impressed by Trump's return to the political stage.

"It's the same old song and dance from Trump," said Moussa. "He's still peddling the same conspiracy theories and lies that he's been pushing for years. It's sad to see the Republican Party still falling for it."

Despite the controversy surrounding his return, Trump's speech in North Carolina made one thing clear: he's not going away anytime soon.

"I'm back, and I'm here to stay," Trump said. "Get used to it, folks. We're going to make America great again, and we're going to do it together."
