Florida Police Arrest Student for Planning School Shooting, Thwarting Potential Tragedy

In a chilling reminder of the ongoing threat of school shootings, a 19-year-old student was arrested in Florida for planning a mass shooting at his university. John Hagins, a student at Embry-Riddle Aeronautical University, was taken into custody by the Daytona Beach Police Department after two students reported his concerning Snapchat messages to authorities.

According to police, Hagins faces charges of terrorism, written threats to injure or kill, and attempted first-degree homicide. He is currently being held without bond at Volusia County Jail.

Daytona Beach Police Chief Jakari Young emphasized the importance of reporting such threats to prevent violence. "We take all threats seriously and will do everything in our power to keep our community safe," he said in a statement.

Hagins' Snapchat messages raised red flags among his classmates, who quickly reported them to authorities. In one message, he referenced the 1999 Columbine High School shooting, stating that he was "finished with his school shopping." He also posted about planning to go to a shooting range for practice and targeted the last day of the semester for maximum impact.

Police found a backpack with ammunition and a folding rifle in Hagins' apartment on campus upon arrest. According to authorities, Hagins had sold his car to buy the weapon and ammunition.

Embry-Riddle Aeronautical University President Barry Butler praised the students and police for their swift action. "We are grateful for the vigilance of our students and the quick response of the Daytona Beach Police Department," he said in a statement. "The safety of our students and staff is our top priority, and we will continue to work closely with law enforcement to ensure our campus remains a safe and secure learning environment."

The arrest comes at a time when school shootings remain a pressing concern in the United States. In recent years, there have been several high-profile incidents, including the Parkland school shooting in Florida in 2018, which left 17 people dead and 17 others injured.

While the motives behind Hagins' actions remain unclear, experts say that early intervention and reporting are crucial in preventing such incidents. "It's important for students to speak up when they hear or see something that doesn't seem right," said Dr. Jillian Peterson, a criminal justice professor at Hamline University. "By reporting these threats, they can help prevent a potential tragedy."
