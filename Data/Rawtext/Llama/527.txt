FEMA Extends Deadline for Union County Residents to Apply for Individual Assistance

Union County, NJ â€“ Union County residents affected by Hurricane Ida have been given an extension until January 5, 2022, to apply for individual assistance from the Federal Emergency Management Agency (FEMA). The FEMA Disaster Recovery Center in Union County will remain open for in-person assistance indefinitely.

"We are grateful for the extension, which will provide additional time for our residents to access the federal assistance they need to recover from Hurricane Ida's devastating impacts," said Alexander Mirabella, Commissioner Board Chairman.

The FEMA Disaster Recovery Center is located at the O'Donnell Dempsey Senior Community Center in Elizabeth, NJ. Operating hours are Monday through Friday, 8:00 a.m. to 6:00 p.m., and Saturdays, 8:00 a.m. to 5:00 p.m.

FEMA disaster assistance may include financial aid for rent, essential home repairs, and other needs not covered by insurance. Applications for disaster assistance can be submitted online, via the FEMA app, or by calling the FEMA Helpline.

In addition to FEMA assistance, Union County residents facing legal issues related to Hurricane Ida can access free legal services. Legal assistance covers benefits, insurance claims, document replacement, and counseling on financial issues.

Union County is among 12 NJ counties designated for FEMA disaster relief following the impacts of Hurricane Ida. Residents are encouraged to take advantage of the available resources to aid in their recovery.

"We understand that the recovery process can be overwhelming, and we want to ensure that our residents have access to all the resources they need to rebuild their lives," said Mirabella.

For more information on FEMA disaster assistance and the Disaster Recovery Center, visit https://www.fema.gov/disaster/4614 or call the FEMA Helpline at 1-800-621-3362.

Union County residents can also contact the Union County Office of Emergency Management at 908-659-7111 for additional information and support.

In conclusion, Union County residents have until January 5, 2022, to apply for individual assistance from FEMA due to Hurricane Ida. The FEMA Disaster Recovery Center in Union County will remain open for in-person assistance indefinitely. Residents are encouraged to take advantage of the available resources to aid in their recovery.
