EVER ACE Makes History: World's Largest Container Ship Crosses Suez Canal on Maiden Voyage

In a groundbreaking feat, the EVER ACE, the world's largest container ship, successfully navigated the Suez Canal on its maiden voyage, solidifying the waterway's reputation as a vital artery for global trade. Built by Samsung Heavy Industries for Evergreen Marine Corporation, the behemoth vessel measures 400 meters in length, 61.53 meters in width, and weighs approximately 235,000 tons.

With a carrying capacity of 23,992 TEU (twenty-foot equivalent units), the EVER ACE has surpassed the HMM Algeciras as the largest container ship in the world. This milestone achievement is a testament to the Suez Canal Authority's (SCA) commitment to accommodating giant container ships, ensuring the safe and efficient passage of cargo through the canal.

The EVER ACE began its maiden voyage from the Port of Taipei, Taiwan, and is headed to Rotterdam, Netherlands. The ship is expected to reach Rotterdam on September 4, marking a significant moment in the shipping industry's history. Evergreen Marine Corporation, the owner of the EVER ACE, also owns the Ever Given, which made headlines earlier this year for blocking the Suez Canal and causing global trade disruptions.

The successful transit of the EVER ACE through the Suez Canal highlights the waterway's capacity to accommodate massive vessels, further solidifying its position as a vital trade route. The SCA provided navigation support to ensure the ship's safe passage, demonstrating their commitment to facilitating the efficient movement of cargo.
