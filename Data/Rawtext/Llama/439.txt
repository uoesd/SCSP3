Harry and Meghan Visit Afghan Refugees at Joint Base McGuire-Dix-Lakehurst

The Duke and Duchess of Sussex, Harry and Meghan, visited Task Force Liberty at Joint Base McGuire-Dix-Lakehurst in New Jersey on Thursday, where they met with Afghan refugees who have recently arrived in the United States. The base is currently home to over 10,000 Afghan refugees.

During their visit, the couple interacted with children in daily schooling, participating in a classroom teaching conversational English. They engaged with the children, using simple English phrases and learning common terms in Dari. Harry and Meghan led the children in singing 'Head, Shoulders, Knees and Toes,' and shared that it's a favorite song of their son, Archie.

The couple thanked the teachers for their efforts and emphasized the importance of self-care and mental health. They also acknowledged the challenges that the refugees have faced and expressed their gratitude for the opportunity to meet with them.

SSgt Jake Carter of Task Force Liberty Public Affairs documented the visit, which highlights Harry and Meghan's ongoing mental health advocacy. The visit comes at a time when the couple has been vocal about their own mental health struggles and the importance of seeking help.

Task Force Liberty was initiated coinciding with the withdrawal of American troops from Afghanistan, aimed at supporting Afghan refugees. The base provides temporary housing, medical care, and other essential services to the refugees until they can be resettled in the United States.

Harry and Meghan's visit to Task Force Liberty is a testament to their commitment to advocating for mental health and supporting vulnerable communities. The couple has used their platform to raise awareness about mental health and reduce stigma around mental illness.
