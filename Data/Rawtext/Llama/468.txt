Oprah's Interview with Harry and Meghan Nominated for Emmy Award

The interview between the Duke and Duchess of Sussex, Prince Harry and Meghan Markle, and media mogul Oprah Winfrey has been nominated for an Emmy Award. The special, which aired on CBS, is up for the Outstanding Hosted Nonfiction Series or Special category.

The Emmy Awards, which will take place on September 20, celebrate excellence in US television. If victorious, Oprah Winfrey and the producing team will receive the award. The special competes against other notable series, including My Next Guest Needs No Introduction with David Letterman.

The interview made headlines worldwide as Prince Harry and Meghan Markle revealed significant allegations against the royal family. Meghan claimed that she was refused help for suicidal thoughts, and both she and Prince Harry expressed concerns about racism within the family, specifically regarding their son Archie's skin tone.

Prince Harry also expressed feeling "let down" by his father, Prince Charles, and mentioned a strained relationship. In response, Buckingham Palace released a statement noting the Queen's sadness and mentioning that "recollections may vary."

The Crown and The Mandalorian lead the Emmy nominations with 24 each. The Crown, a Netflix series that follows the life of Queen Elizabeth II, has been nominated for several awards, including Outstanding Drama Series. The Mandalorian, a Disney+ series set in the Star Wars universe, has also received several nominations, including Outstanding Drama Series.

Other notable nominees include Stranger Things, The Handmaid's Tale, and Saturday Night Live. The 72nd Emmy Awards will be hosted by Jimmy Kimmel and will air live on ABC.

In conclusion, the interview between Oprah Winfrey and the Duke and Duchess of Sussex has been nominated for an Emmy Award, highlighting the significance of the revelations made during the special. The Emmy Awards celebrate the best in US television, and the nomination is a testament to the impact of the interview.
