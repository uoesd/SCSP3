Brady's Big Splash: NFL Quarterback Purchases $6 Million Yacht

Tom Brady, the legendary quarterback for the Tampa Bay Buccaneers, has made a splash in the boating world with his recent purchase of a $6 million yacht. This luxurious vessel is an upgrade from his previous $2 million Wajer 55S yacht, which he brought to the Bucs' boat parade after their Super Bowl victory in February.

Brady's love for yachts began when he moved from the New England Patriots to Tampa Bay, which he described as a "boating community." In an interview, he expressed his excitement about his new purchase, stating, "I'm really looking forward to spending time on the water with my family. The Wajer 77 is the perfect yacht for day and weekend trips, especially to the Bahamas."

The Wajer 77 is indeed a step up from Brady's previous yacht, offering more space and amenities for family vacations. With a length of 77 feet, it boasts three cabins, a spacious salon, and an expansive outdoor area perfect for entertaining. While Brady's yacht purchase may be making headlines, another piece of football history is up for grabs. The football from Brady's first pass in the NFL is being auctioned off by Lelands, offering fans a chance to own a piece of the quarterback's legacy.

Jordan Gilroy, director of acquisitions at Lelands, highlighted the rarity and potential future value increase of the football. "This is an incredible opportunity for football fans to own a piece of history," Gilroy said. "The fact that it's Tom Brady's first pass in the NFL makes it even more special."

Gilroy also mentioned the possibility of the football going into a private collection, potentially making this auction its last public offering. "We're excited to see how high the bidding will go," he said. "This is truly a once-in-a-lifetime chance for football enthusiasts."
