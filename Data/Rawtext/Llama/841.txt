Tiger Woods' Celebrity Golf Series Premieres Despite Car Crash Setback

In a surprising turn of events, Tiger Woods' new miniseries, 'A Round with Tiger: Celebrity Lessons,' has premiered despite his recent car crash in California. The series, which features celebrities like Jada Pinkett Smith, David Spade, and Dwyane Wade, was filmed in February, just one day before Woods' accident.

According to Woods, the series was filmed with his blessing, and he is grateful for the support he has received during this difficult time. In the premiere episode, Woods sits down with Jada Pinkett Smith to discuss the challenges of aging and how it has impacted his body.

Woods has undergone 11 major surgeries, including an emergency surgery for his February 23 car accident injuries. Despite the setbacks, Woods remains hopeful about his recovery and return to golf.

Dwyane Wade, one of Woods' celebrity guests on the show, expressed his shock at Woods' accident and hopes for his speedy recovery. "I'm just glad he's okay," Wade said. "I know how much golf means to him, and I hope he can get back out there soon."

Woods described his recovery from the car crash as "more painful than anything I have ever experienced." Despite the pain, he remains focused on his recovery and appreciates the support he has received from fans and friends alike.

The Los Angeles County Sheriff's department determined that speed was the primary cause of Woods' accident, and he wasn't cited. Woods is taking the necessary steps to ensure his safety and the safety of others on the road.

'A Round with Tiger: Celebrity Playing Lessons' airs Tuesdays on Golf Digest through August 10. Fans can tune in to see Woods teach celebrities the ins and outs of golf, all while sharing his own experiences and insights.

In a statement, Woods said, "I'm thrilled to share my passion for golf with these amazing celebrities. It's been a challenging year, but I'm grateful for the support and can't wait to get back out there."
