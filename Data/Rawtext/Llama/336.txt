Ever Given: One of the World's Largest Container Ships Partially Refloated in Suez Canal

One of the world's largest container ships, the Ever Given, has been partially refloated in the Suez Canal after running aground due to loss of steering capability amid high winds and a dust storm. The incident has caused a major disruption to international trade, with a growing number of tankers, carrying millions of barrels of crude oil, waiting to pass through the canal.

According to Jamil Sayegh, a former captain and maritime law specialist, the Ever Given's technical manager, Bernhard Schulte Shipmanagement, has confirmed that all crew members are safe. However, the ship remains stuck in the canal, blocking a crucial lane and affecting Asia-Europe trade.

The Suez Canal is a vital artery for international trade, with about 50 ships passing through it every day in 2019. The blockage caused by the Ever Given has already had a significant impact on trade, with Peter Sand, chief shipping analyst at Bimco, stating that "the incident highlights the importance of the Suez Canal as a critical chokepoint for global trade."

Lars Jensen, chief executive of SeaIntelligence Consulting, noted that strong winds may have caused the incident, turning the containers into a vast sail. He also suggested that human error and the necessity of not delaying shipments might have contributed to the accident.

The Ever Given is not the first ship to run into trouble in the Suez Canal. In 2017, a Japanese ship ran aground in the canal, while the CSCL Indian Ocean, a container ship, suffered a similar fate near Hamburg in 2016.

The partial refloating of the Ever Given is a positive development, but it remains to be seen how long it will take to fully free the ship and restore normal traffic in the canal. The incident has already highlighted the importance of the Suez Canal as a vital trade route and the need for vigilance to prevent such incidents in the future.

As the world waits anxiously for the Ever Given to be fully refloated, it is clear that this incident will have a lasting impact on international trade and the shipping industry as a whole. The Suez Canal will continue to play a critical role in global trade, and it is essential that measures are taken to prevent such incidents from happening again.
