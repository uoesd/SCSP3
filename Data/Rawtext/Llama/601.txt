"I think we need to be more targeted in who we're giving the money to," Manchin said. "We've already invested $4.8 trillion in response to the pandemic, more than the entire U.S. budget. We need to make sure we're not just giving money to people who don't need it."

Vice President Harris defended the administration's approach, emphasizing the need to support all Americans during this difficult time.

"We're not just talking about a stimulus package, we're talking about a rescue plan," Harris said. "We're committed to making sure that every American has access to the resources they need to get through this pandemic."

The difference in approach between Sen. Manchin and the Biden administration highlights ongoing intra-party debate on how best to provide economic relief to Americans. While Manchin has called for a more targeted approach, the administration has emphasized the need to support all Americans, regardless of their financial situation.

Harris echoed this sentiment, emphasizing the administration's commitment to working with both parties to pass the ARP.
