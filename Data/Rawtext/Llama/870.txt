Rory McIlroy Visits Tiger Woods, Shares Insights into Woods' Focus on Majors

In an interview with Golf.com, McIlroy shared details of their conversation, revealing Woods' unique perspective on major trophies versus other achievements.

According to McIlroy, Woods is in "decent spirits" despite the severity of the crash. "He's doing okay, considering what he's been through," McIlroy said. "He's still got that competitive fire in his belly, which is great to see."

During their conversation, Woods shared an anecdote that highlighted his focus on major wins. McIlroy recalled, "Tiger told me that he values his major trophies more than any other tournament wins. He said, 'I've got 82 PGA Tour event trophies, but my 15 major trophies are the ones that really matter.'"

This revelation underscores Woods' selective valuation of tournament wins, emphasizing the importance he places on majors. For Woods, winning the Masters, the U.S. Open, the Open Championship, and the PGA Championship holds greater significance than any other tournament.

McIlroy expressed admiration for Woods' legacy and competitive mindset, acknowledging the impact Woods has had on his own career. "Tiger's been a huge influence on me, and I'm sure he has been for many other golfers," McIlroy said. "His drive and determination are something to behold. I've learned a lot from him, and I'm grateful for the opportunity to call him a friend."

McIlroy hopes for Woods' recovery and sees a possibility of Woods returning to competitive golf, perhaps for the 2022 Masters. "I think he's got a few more majors left in him," McIlroy said. "He's been through a lot, but he's a fighter. I wouldn't count him out."

Justin Thomas, another pro golfer, also visited Woods and shared his disappointment about Woods missing the Masters. "It's tough to see him not playing, especially at Augusta," Thomas said. "But we all want what's best for Tiger, and if that means taking the time he needs to recover, then so be it."

Thomas mentioned the inspiration he and other younger golfers draw from Woods, highlighting a sense of community among players. "Tiger's been an icon in the sport for so long," Thomas said. "He's paved the way for all of us, and we're grateful for everything he's done."

McIlroy sees a collective responsibility among golfers to support Woods and aid his return to the sport. "We all want to see Tiger back out there, competing at the highest level," McIlroy said. "We'll do whatever we can to help him get there."

As Woods continues his recovery, the golf community remains hopeful for his return. With the Masters just around the corner, players and fans alike are eager to see the sport's greatest competitor back in action.

Despite the uncertainty surrounding Woods' future, one thing is clear: his legacy continues to inspire and motivate golfers around the world. As McIlroy put it, "Tiger's impact on the sport is immeasurable. He's a true champion, and we're all rooting for him."
