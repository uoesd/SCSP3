Gabby Petito's Van Spotted in Travel Blogger's Footage, Leading to FBI Tip

The footage was featured in an episode of ABC's '20/20' titled "Life on Line: The Last Days of Gabby Petito."

Bethune's footage was captured in August 2020, just days before Petito was reported missing. In an interview with '20/20,' Bethune explained that she had been filming her trip and didn't realize the significance of the van until she saw a news report about Petito's disappearance. She immediately contacted the FBI and provided them with her footage.

The episode of '20/20' explores the last days of Petito and Laundrie's lives, including the discovery of Laundrie's remains in the Carlton Reserve. The program features interviews with experts on body language, legal analysts, and former Moab police chief Jim Winder, who analyzed police body cam footage.

Winder, who retired from the Moab Police Department in 2019, spoke to '20/20' about the investigation into Petito's disappearance. "We knew that Gabby was missing, and we knew that Brian was the last person to see her," he said. "We wanted to talk to him, but he refused to cooperate."

The episode also includes an interview with Miranda Baker, who encountered Laundrie at a gas station in Moab. Baker described Laundrie as acting suspiciously and said that he left the gas station in a hurry.

Jason Jensen, a private investigator and co-founder of the Cold Case Coalition, also spoke to '20/20' about the case. Jensen, who was hired by Petito's family to investigate her disappearance, believes that Laundrie was responsible for her death. "I think Brian knew that Gabby was going to leave him, and he couldn't handle it," he said.

Mykel Hawke, a retired special forces combat veteran and survival expert, guided '20/20' through the Carlton Reserve, where Laundrie's body was found. Hawke explained that the reserve is a vast and rugged area, making it a challenging place to search for a missing person.
