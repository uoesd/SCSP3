9:29: The Corrected Timing of George Floyd's Kneeling Raises Questions of Police Brutality

CNN – Minneapolis, Minnesota – The trial of former Officer Derek Chauvin has brought to light new details surrounding the death of George Floyd, including the corrected timing of Chauvin's kneeling on Floyd's neck. According to prosecutors, Chauvin knelt on Floyd for 9 minutes and 29 seconds, a duration that has sparked debate and questions of police brutality.

In his opening statements, Prosecuting Attorney Jerry Blackwell emphasized the significance of the 9:29 timing, stating, "This case is not about a split-second decision made by a police officer in the heat of the moment. This case is about a deliberate and unnecessary use of force that lasted for 9 minutes and 29 seconds."

The 9:29 timing has corrected the previously thought duration of 8:46, which has become a symbol of police brutality and systemic racism. Despite the correction, the 8:46 timing has been widely commemorated, including by Floyd's family, and has inspired protests, moments of silence, and creative works.

The kneeling time was broken down into three sections, showing Floyd's distress and eventual non-responsiveness. Chauvin's defense accepts the revised timing and argues it supports their case, claiming that Floyd was resisting arrest and that Chauvin's actions were necessary to subdue him.

However, the altercation in the trial has been less about the timing difference and more about the extensive evidence presented, including witness testimonies and video footage. The Hennepin County Attorney's Office has corrected the timing on several occasions, citing initial errors in reporting the duration due to the timing of viral bystander video.

Defense Attorney Eric Nelson has acknowledged the revised timing but argues that it does not change the fact that Floyd was resisting arrest. Nelson stated, "The fact remains that Mr. Floyd was not compliant with the officers' instructions, and Officer Chauvin's actions were necessary to effect a lawful arrest."

The corrected timing of 9:29 has raised questions about the use of force by police officers and the need for reform. Blackwell argued, "The evidence will show that Officer Chauvin's actions were not only unnecessary but also unreasonable, and that he violated George Floyd's constitutional rights."
