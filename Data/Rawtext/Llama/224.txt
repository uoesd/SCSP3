REvil Ransomware Group Hacked and Deactivated in Multinational Operation

In a major blow to the notorious ransomware group REvil, a multinational cyber operation has successfully hacked and deactivated their systems, using a similar technique that REvil employed to compromise targets. The operation involved the FBI and investigative arms from multiple countries, alongside the U.S. Cyber Command.

REvil, also known as Sodinokibi, has been linked to several high-profile attacks, including the hack of the U.S. Colonial Pipeline in May, which caused significant fuel shortages and led to a state of emergency being declared across 17 states. The group's main spokesperson, known as Unknown, disappeared from the internet in July, leading to speculation about the group's fate.

According to a post on an internet forum by a leader of REvil known as 0_neday, the group's servers were compromised by law enforcement agencies, who were able to access and disable their systems from the inside. 0_neday confirmed that the server compromise was the result of a vulnerability in REvil's backup system, which was exploited by the operation.

Oleg Skulkin, an official from the Russian security company Group-IB, commented on the irony of REvil's tactics being used against them. "It's a classic case of 'live by the sword, die by the sword'," Skulkin said. "REvil's own tactics have been turned against them, and it's a reminder that no one is immune to cyber attacks, not even notorious ransomware groups."

The shutdown maneuver involved law enforcement agencies accessing and disabling REvil's servers from the inside, effectively cutting off the group's ability to communicate with their victims and demand ransom payments. While the operation has deactivated REvil's systems, it remains to be seen whether the group's members will face criminal charges or if they will regroup and continue their activities under a different name.

The U.S. government has ramped up efforts to treat ransomware attacks on critical infrastructure as national security threats, following the Colonial Pipeline hack by REvil and DarkSide. The attack on software management company Kaseya, which was also attributed to REvil, further highlighted the need for a coordinated response to these types of attacks.

The success of the multinational operation against REvil is a significant blow to the ransomware ecosystem and a warning to other groups that they are not invincible. It also underscores the importance of international cooperation in tackling cyber threats, as ransomware attacks continue to pose a major threat to businesses and critical infrastructure worldwide.

In a statement, a spokesperson for the FBI said, "We are committed to working with our international partners to disrupt and dismantle ransomware groups like REvil, who pose a significant threat to our national security and the safety of our citizens."

The operation against REvil is just the latest example of law enforcement agencies taking a more aggressive stance against ransomware groups. In recent months, the U.S. government has launched a number of initiatives aimed at combating ransomware, including the creation of a new task force dedicated to investigating and prosecuting ransomware attacks.
