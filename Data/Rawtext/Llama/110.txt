Chris Cuomo Advises Brother Andrew Cuomo to Resign Amidst Sexual Harassment Scandal

In a surprising turn of events, CNN anchor Chris Cuomo has advised his brother, New York Governor Andrew Cuomo, to resign amidst a sexual harassment scandal. Chris addressed the issue upon returning to his show "Cuomo Primetime" after a vacation, stating that he never covered the scandal on CNN due to a conflict of interest but did offer his brother personal advice.

Chris Cuomo's advice to Andrew included owning his actions, being contrite, and realizing that perceptions of his actions matter more than intent. This comes as a damning report by the Attorney General found that Andrew Cuomo sexually harassed 11 women, violating state and federal laws.

Andrew Cuomo announced his resignation, effective in 14 days from his announcement, following the release of the report. This move comes after months of speculation and mounting pressure for the governor to step down.

The sexual harassment scandal is not the only controversy surrounding Andrew Cuomo. There is an ongoing federal investigation into his handling of COVID-19 in nursing homes and another by the Attorney General into the misuse of state resources for his book.

Chris Cuomo emphasized his commitment to doing the right thing and maintaining professional integrity despite the unique situation. In May, he acknowledged discussing advisory strategies with his brother and his staff, a move he later apologized for after CNN expressed it was inappropriate.

Despite the conflict of interest, Chris Cuomo maintained that he never misled anyone on his show nor attempted to control CNN's coverage of his family. He has been praised for his commitment to journalistic integrity and for not shying away from covering difficult topics.

The Cuomo brothers have been known for their close relationship, with Chris often interviewing Andrew on his show. However, Chris has made it clear that he will not compromise his journalistic integrity for the sake of family ties.
