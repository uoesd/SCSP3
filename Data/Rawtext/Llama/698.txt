Michigan High School Shooting Leaves Four Dead, 15-Year-Old Charged with Terrorism

A tragic shooting at Oxford High School in Michigan has left four students dead and a 15-year-old suspect, Ethan Crumbley, charged with 24 crimes, including terrorism causing death. The community is reeling from the loss and grappling with the impact of the tragedy.

Austin Witt, a senior at Oxford High, remembered victim Tate Myre as the "heart and soul" of their community. "He was always there for everyone, no matter what," Witt said. "He had a smile on his face every day, and he was always willing to lend a helping hand."

Investigators found digital evidence suggesting Crumbley planned the attack, including a video and journal detailing his intent. A social media post showed the gun used in the attack, which was purchased four days before the incident.

Oakland County prosecutor Karen McDonald emphasized the importance of charging Crumbley as an adult, stating, "It's essential for justice, and it's essential for the safety of our community." McDonald highlighted the trauma experienced by surviving students as a reason for the serious charges.

The shooting began when Crumbley emerged from a bathroom with a gun and began firing in the hallway. Senior Mitchell Williams described the event as "horrific," underlining the confusion and fear during the shooting. "I was in class when we heard the shots," Williams said. "We didn't know what was happening. We thought it was a drill at first, but then we saw the police rushing in, and we knew it was real."

The community is struggling to come to terms with the loss of life and the impact of the tragedy. "It's hard to put into words how this feels," Witt said. "It's like a nightmare that we can't wake up from. We're all just trying to support each other and get through this together."

The shooting has sparked a renewed debate about gun control and school safety. "This is a wake-up call for all of us," McDonald said. "We need to take action to prevent these kinds of tragedies from happening again. We owe it to the victims and their families to do better."

As the community mourns the loss of life, they are also grappling with the fact that the suspect is only 15 years old. "It's hard to comprehend how someone so young could do something like this," Williams said. "It's a reminder that we need to pay attention to the mental health of our young people and make sure they're getting the help they need."
