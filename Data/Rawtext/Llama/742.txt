Alec Baldwin Speaks Out for First Time Since Fatal 'Rust' Movie Set Shooting

Alec Baldwin has broken his silence on the tragic shooting that occurred on the set of his upcoming movie, "Rust," in Vermont. The actor spoke publicly for the first time since the incident, expressing his cooperation with the police and his commitment to ensuring such a tragedy never happens again.

In an interview with Fox 5 DC, Baldwin emphasized that the shooting, which resulted in the death of his friend and cinematographer Halyna Hutchins, was a "one in a trillion event." "My heart is broken for Halyna and her family," Baldwin said. "I'm interested in limiting the use of real firearms on set to prevent future incidents. Whatever decisions are made to protect people on film sets, I'm in favor of."

"This is a tragedy that should never have happened," Mendoza said. "We're going to get to the bottom of it and find out what went wrong. We owe it to Halyna and her family to ensure that something like this never happens again."

Baldwin echoed the sheriff's sentiments, expressing his commitment to ensuring that safety measures are put in place to prevent such a tragedy from occurring in the future.
