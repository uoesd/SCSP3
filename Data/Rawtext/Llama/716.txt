Arrest Made After 17-Year-Old Brings Handgun to Detroit-Area School

A day after a deadly school shooting at Oxford High School, a 17-year-old boy was arrested for bringing a handgun to Southfield Regional Academic Campus in the Detroit area. The arrest was made after the school principal alerted a school resource officer following a tip about a student possibly carrying a gun.

According to Southfield Police Chief Elvin Barren, a purple Bersa Thunder .380-caliber gun with a magazine containing three bullets was found in the student's coat pocket. The chief stated that the student had likely been carrying the weapon for several weeks.

The arrested teen claimed he found the weapon and had no rounds loaded in the gun's chamber. However, authorities do not believe the incident was a copycat of the Oxford High School shooting.

Chief Barren praised the student who reported the gun to the principal for his courageous action, stating, "We commend this young man for coming forward and reporting this incident. His actions likely prevented a potentially dangerous situation from unfolding."

The gun will be examined to see if it has been used in crimes, and the prosecutor's office will decide on charging the teen. Chief Barren warned of the false sense of power guns can give to individuals, leading to potential violence over disagreements.

"We must remain vigilant in our efforts to keep our schools and communities safe," Chief Barren said. "Guns can give individuals a false sense of power, leading to potential violence over disagreements. We must work together to prevent such incidents from occurring."

The incident has left parents and students shaken, with many expressing concerns about the prevalence of guns in schools. "It's terrifying to think that a student could bring a gun to school and potentially harm others," said one parent. "We need to do more to ensure the safety of our children."

The school has increased security measures in response to the incident, with additional police officers present on campus and metal detectors being installed. "We take the safety and security of our students very seriously," said the school principal. "We will do everything in our power to prevent such incidents from happening in the future."

The incident serves as a reminder of the importance of vigilance in maintaining school safety. As Chief Barren noted, "We must all work together to prevent gun violence in our schools and communities. We must remain vigilant and report any suspicious activity to the authorities."
