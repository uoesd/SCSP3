Tragedy Strikes Utah Football Again as Player Aaron Lowe Fatally Shot

In a devastating turn of events, Utah football player Aaron Lowe was fatally shot early Sunday in Salt Lake City, less than a year after his teammate and friend Ty Jordan was killed in an accidental shooting. Lowe, who was in his third season with the Utes, was named the first recipient of the Ty Jordan Memorial Scholarship just last week.

According to Salt Lake City police, the shooting occurred at a house party in the early hours of Sunday morning. A second person, an unidentified woman, was also shot and is currently in critical condition. Police received calls about a noise complaint and a fight involving a weapon at the party, but no arrests have been made in connection to the shooting.

Utah coach Kyle Whittingham expressed his devastation over Lowe's passing, saying in a statement, "We are deeply saddened by the loss of Aaron Lowe. Our thoughts and prayers are with his family and friends. Aaron was a leader on our team and his presence will be greatly missed."

Utah athletic director Mark Harlan also praised Lowe, saying, "Aaron was an exceptional young man and a leader on our football team. We are heartbroken by his loss and our thoughts and prayers are with his family and friends. We will do everything we can to support them during this difficult time."

Salt Lake City Police Chief Mike Brown expressed his condolences, saying, "Our hearts go out to the family and friends of Aaron Lowe. We are working diligently to identify the suspect or suspects involved in this tragic shooting and bring them to justice."

Utah Gov. Spencer Cox also offered his condolences, saying, "We are deeply saddened by the loss of Aaron Lowe. Our thoughts and prayers are with his family, friends, and the entire Utah football community. We will do everything we can to support them during this difficult time."

Detectives are currently working to identify a suspect or suspects involved in the shooting. Anyone with information is urged to come forward and contact the Salt Lake City Police Department.

Lowe's death has sent shockwaves through the Utah football community, which is still reeling from the loss of Ty Jordan last year. Jordan, a freshman running back, was accidentally shot and killed by a friend in December 2020. Lowe's scholarship, which was established in Jordan's memory, will now be awarded to a different player in his honor.
