The Federal Energy Regulatory Commission (FERC) has released a preliminary report, pointing to freezing or insufficient winterizing of generators as the main cause of the outages.

According to the report, 44% of outage issues were due to freezing, while 31% were caused by natural gas fuel shortages. Most natural gas production and processing facilities were not protected from load shedding, leading to a decline in supply.

"The report highlights the need for better winterization of our power infrastructure," said Daniel Cohan, Associate Professor of Environmental Engineering at Rice University. "It's a reminder that our power grid is vulnerable to extreme weather events, and we need to take proactive steps to protect it."

The FERC report offers 28 recommendations, focusing on winterizing equipment and conducting annual winterization training. However, a similar report was issued in 2011, but most recommendations were not implemented by power providers.

"It's frustrating to see the same issues arising again and again," said Ramanan Krishnamoorti, Chief Energy Officer at the University of Houston. "We need to take these recommendations seriously and invest in the necessary upgrades to ensure the reliability of our power grid."

Experts emphasize the importance of adhering to the new recommendations to avoid repeat occurrences of power outages. The Texas power grid's improvement requires making economic incentives for energy producers to enhance reliability.

In response to the report's findings, ERCOT is working with the PUC to implement legislated mandates and improve grid reliability. The Federal Energy Regulatory Commission's full report and recommendations are available on their website.

The winter freeze disaster has highlighted the need for a more resilient power grid in Texas. As the state continues to grow and extreme weather events become more frequent, it's crucial that power providers take proactive steps to protect the grid and ensure the safety of millions of Texans.
