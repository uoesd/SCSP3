Waters' comments drew immediate criticism from Republicans, who claimed she was inciting violence. Senate Minority Leader Mitch McConnell (R-KY) sharply criticized Waters, with some calling for her censure. House Minority Leader Kevin McCarthy (R-CA) announced he is introducing a resolution to censure Waters for her "dangerous comments."

However, Waters clarified that her comment about being "confrontational" refers to nonviolent actions and speaking up for justice system reform. "I'm not talking about violence, I'm talking about confronting the system," she said.

Judge Peter Cahill, who is presiding over Chauvin's trial, suggested that Waters' comments could be grounds for an appeal in the case. House Speaker Nancy Pelosi (D-CA) defended Waters, denying the need for an apology and dismissing claims that Waters incited violence.

House Majority Leader Steny Hoyer (D-MD) downplayed Waters' remarks but expressed concern over their potential impact on the trial's appeal. "I don't think it's helpful to make comments that could be interpreted as inciting violence or confrontation," he said.

The controversy over Waters' comments contrasts with GOP responses to the Capitol insurrection and previous statements by Rep. Marjorie Taylor Greene (R-GA). Greene has faced criticism for her comments supporting conspiracy theories and violence against Democrats, but has not faced censure from her party.

Waters has been a vocal advocate for police reform and justice for George Floyd, who was killed by Chauvin during a police arrest last May. Protests have continued in Brooklyn Center and across the country, calling for justice and systemic change.

In a statement, Waters said, "I understand that my words may have caused concern, but I want to be clear: I am not advocating for violence or harm towards anyone. My intention was to encourage people to continue to fight for justice and equality, peacefully and respectfully."

The controversy over Waters' comments has sparked a larger debate about the role of protest and activism in shaping public policy. While some argue that confrontational tactics are necessary to bring attention to systemic issues, others believe that nonviolent methods are more effective in achieving lasting change.

As the trial of Derek Chauvin continues, the focus remains on ensuring justice for George Floyd and addressing the systemic issues that led to his killing. The controversy over Waters' comments serves as a reminder of the importance of respectful dialogue and peaceful activism in shaping a more just and equitable society.
