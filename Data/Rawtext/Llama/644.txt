RIOT DECLARED IN PORTLAND AS PROTESTERS REACT TO KYLE RITTENHOUSE ACQUITTAL

Portland, OR - Law enforcement in Portland have declared a riot as around 200 demonstrators took to the streets to protest the acquittal of Kyle Rittenhouse, the man who killed two people and injured another during a Wisconsin protest against police brutality.

The protests in Portland turned violent, with demonstrators breaking windows, throwing objects at police, and talking about burning down a government building. Several people were given citations, and one person was arrested on an outstanding warrant.

Tanya Watkins, executive director of Southsiders Organized for Unity and Liberation, expressed her disappointment and rage at the verdict, stating, "We are outraged and devastated by the acquittal of Kyle Rittenhouse. This verdict is a slap in the face to the families of the victims and to all those who are fighting for justice and equality."

The protests in Portland were just one of several that took place across the United States, with demonstrations also occurring in New York, Los Angeles, and Chicago. In Chicago, around 1,000 people marched in a protest organized by Black Lives Matter Chicago and other groups.

Portland Police Bureau Chief Chuck Lovell stated that officers were prepared for the protests following the Rittenhouse verdict. "We have been working closely with our community partners and have taken steps to ensure the safety of everyone involved," he said.

Despite this, some demonstrators in Portland threw urine, water bottles, and batteries at deputies. The police responded with riot control measures, including tear gas and rubber bullets.
