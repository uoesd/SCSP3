Ever Given container ship freed from Suez Canal after five-day blockage

The Ever Given container ship was finally freed from the Suez Canal on Tuesday, five days after it became lodged in the waterway, causing a massive traffic jam of over 360 ships. The blockage was a significant disruption to global trade, potentially costing billions in delayed shipments.

The Ever Given, operated by Evergreen Marine of Taiwan, was traveling through the canal when it became stuck on March 23. High winds during a sandstorm were initially blamed for the incident, but technical or human errors are also being investigated.

The blockage forced some ships to take a longer route around Africa, raising concerns about piracy. The Suez Canal is a critical maritime link, and the blockage threatened significant economic impacts worldwide. Egypt operates the Suez Canal, which contributed $5.6 billion to its revenue in 2020.
