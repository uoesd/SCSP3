Derek Chauvin Seeks New Trial Over George Floyd's Death, Citing Jury and Prosecutorial Misconduct

Derek Chauvin, the former Minneapolis police officer convicted of unintentional second-degree murder, third-degree murder, and second-degree manslaughter in the death of George Floyd, is seeking a new trial, citing several errors in the original trial, including jury and prosecutorial misconduct, and a verdict contrary to law.
