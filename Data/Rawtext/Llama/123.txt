Kathy Hochul Sworn in as New York's First Female Governor, Vows to Restore Ethics in Albany

Kathy Hochul was sworn in as the first female governor of New York on Tuesday, succeeding Andrew Cuomo who resigned amid multiple scandals. Hochul, a former lieutenant governor under Cuomo since 2015, seeks to distance herself from his administration and restore ethics in Albany.

"I want to assure all New Yorkers that I will not tolerate any unethical behavior in my administration," Hochul said in her inaugural address. "I will remove any aides implicated in unethical practices as reported by the state attorney general."

Hochul's pledge to restore ethics in Albany comes at a time when the state government has been marred by scandals, including sexual harassment allegations against Cuomo and criticism over his handling of COVID-19 in nursing homes.

"The Cuomo administration's lack of transparency and accountability has led to a culture of corruption and abuse of power," said Erica Vladimer, co-founder of the Sexual Harassment Working Group. "Governor Hochul has a unique opportunity to change the status quo and create a safe and equitable workplace for all New Yorkers."

Hochul faces numerous challenges as she takes office, including the ongoing COVID-19 pandemic, the distribution of rental assistance, and the potential extension of an eviction moratorium.

"Governor Hochul has a lot on her plate, but she has the opportunity to make a real difference in the lives of New Yorkers," said John Kaehny, executive director of Reinvent Albany. "She should prioritize transparency and accountability in her administration and work to pass legislation that supports worker protections and oversight."

One potential conflict of interest that has already arisen involves Hochul's husband, William Hochul Jr., who works for Delaware North, a company that has been awarded state contracts. Hochul has emphasized her commitment to ethics and has taken measures to prevent conflicts of interest regarding her husband's position.

"I understand the concerns about my husband's employment, but I want to assure New Yorkers that I have taken every necessary step to prevent any conflicts of interest," Hochul said. "I will continue to work closely with the state's ethics commission to ensure that my administration is transparent and accountable."

Hochul's political background includes being a moderate Democrat with roots in conservative Western New York. Her tenure will likely face scrutiny from both progressives and potential gubernatorial candidates as the next election approaches.

"Governor Hochul has a chance to prove herself as a leader who is committed to ethics and transparency," said Kenneth Sherrill, professor emeritus of political science at Hunter College. "She will need to navigate the challenges of governing in a state with a complex political landscape, but she has the opportunity to make a positive impact on the lives of New Yorkers."

As Hochul begins her tenure as governor, she has promised to work tirelessly to address the challenges facing the state.

"I am committed to serving the people of New York and restoring trust in our state government," Hochul said. "I will work every day to ensure that our state is a place where everyone can thrive and succeed."
