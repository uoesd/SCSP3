Russia Sees Surge in Social Media Activity During Facebook Outage

MOSCOW, Oct 5 â€“ Russia's social networks reported a significant increase in activity during Facebook's global outage, which officials claim supports their efforts to develop sovereign internet platforms.

According to Russia's Foreign Ministry spokesperson Maria Zakharova, the outage highlights the need for Russia to have its own social networks. "We need to have our own platforms, our own social networks, our own messengers," she said.

Vkontakte, Russia's largest social network, saw an 18% increase in video views and a 21% increase in messages during the Facebook outage. The platform's audience also increased by 19% compared to the day before the outage. Odnoklassniki, another popular Russian social network, also reported increased activity during the outage.

The surge in activity on Russian social networks comes as Russia has been pressuring foreign tech firms to comply with its internet regulations. Earlier this year, Russia conducted tests to disconnect from the global internet, part of its effort to protect itself from being cut off from foreign internet infrastructure.

Facebook's outage, which affected 3.5 billion users, was caused by a faulty configuration change. The company has since resolved the issue, but the incident has raised concerns about the reliance on a single platform for communication and information sharing.

Marina Krasnova, head of Vkontakte, said that the outage demonstrated the importance of having multiple social media platforms. "This situation shows that it's important to have alternative platforms, so that people can continue to communicate and share information," she said.

The increased activity on Russian social networks during the Facebook outage highlights the growing importance of these platforms in Russia. As the country continues to develop its sovereign internet, it's likely that we'll see more emphasis on homegrown social media platforms.

In conclusion, while Facebook's outage was a global issue, it's clear that Russia's social networks are becoming increasingly important for communication and information sharing within the country. As Russia continues to develop its sovereign internet, we can expect to see more growth and development in this area.
