Vice President Kamala Harris' Interview on 'The View' Delayed Due to COVID-19 Scare

Vice President Kamala Harris' scheduled appearance on ABC's 'The View' was delayed on Thursday after co-hosts Sunny Hostin and Ana Navarro tested positive for COVID-19. Despite being fully vaccinated, the two hosts experienced "breakthrough" infections and abruptly left the show.

In light of the situation, Harris conducted the interview remotely within the studio's building to ensure safety. Joy Behar, co-host of 'The View', informed viewers of the situation and reassured measures to keep Vice President Harris safe.

During the interview, Vice President Harris emphasized the significance of vaccinations, attributing the non-severe status of the co-hosts to being vaccinated. She stated, "We are grateful that they are vaccinated, and that's why they're not experiencing severe symptoms."

Deputy press secretary Sabrina Singh confirmed that Harris hadn't contacted the hosts prior to the show and her schedule would proceed as planned. Singh said, "The Vice President did not have any contact with the hosts before the show, and her schedule will continue as planned."

Despite the disruption, Harris addressed crucial issues like COVID-19, immigration, and foreign aid during her appearance. She criticized the aggressive actions of border agents against Haitian migrants, likening it to dark episodes of American history. Harris emphasized the importance of accountability and consequences for the agents involved. She also called for increased assistance for Haiti to help the nation recover from natural disasters and political instability.

In conclusion, Vice President Kamala Harris' interview on 'The View' was delayed due to COVID-19 concerns, but she still managed to address important issues and emphasize the importance of vaccinations. The incident serves as a reminder of the ongoing pandemic and the need for continued vigilance.
