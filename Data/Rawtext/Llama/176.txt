Climate Change Disrupts Earth's Water Cycle, Leading to Extreme Weather Events

As the Earth's climate continues to warm, the water cycle is being significantly disrupted, leading to more extreme weather events, such as droughts, floods, and heatwaves. This is the warning being sounded by climate scientists, including Peter Gleick, co-founder of the Pacific Institute in Oakland, California.

Gleick has testified before Congress on the risks posed by climate change, particularly to agriculture and access to clean water. His research over the years has predicted many of the changes that are now being observed, and he expressed a sense of "I told you so" regarding the predictions made by climate scientists. However, Gleick notes that the recognition of these changes serves as vindication for past climate science work, but also highlights the sadness in realizing that earlier policy actions could have mitigated current impacts.

The changes in the climate and water cycles are having severe impacts, with increased temperatures resulting in higher water demand and faster evaporation from reservoirs. Gleick points out that the longstanding and increasing global temperatures are a major factor in these changes.
