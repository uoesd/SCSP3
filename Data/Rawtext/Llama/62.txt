US House Committee Issues Subpoenas to Six Former Trump Associates in Capitol Riot Probe

In a significant development in the ongoing investigation into the January 6 Capitol riot, the US House of Representatives committee has issued subpoenas to six former Trump associates. The committee is seeking testimony and documents from these individuals, who are accused of helping Donald Trump spread false claims about election fraud and being involved in efforts to overturn the 2020 presidential elections.

According to Representative Bennie Thompson, the Chair of the House committee, the subpoenas were issued to Bill Stepien, Jason Miller, Angela McCallum, John Eastman, Michael Flynn, and Bernard Kerik. Thompson stated that the committee is investigating misinformation about the election and plans to prevent the Electoral College vote count.

The subpoenas come after Trump was impeached for incitement of insurrection following the storming of the US Capitol by his supporters. The panel is demanding testimony and documents from former Trump campaign officials and others who partook in efforts to stop the certification of President Joe Biden's election victory.

White House Press Secretary Jen Psaki emphasized the administration's cooperation with investigations to prevent such events in the future. She stated that the Biden administration refused Trump's attempt to use executive privilege to block access to White House documents related to the riot.

The House panel has already interviewed over 150 people across government, social media, and law enforcement. Some former Trump advisers, including Steve Bannon, have resisted testifying in the probe.

The investigation is focused on uncovering the truth behind the Capitol riot and ensuring that such events do not occur in the future. The committee is working to gather all relevant information and hold those responsible accountable for their actions.

The issuance of subpoenas to six former Trump associates marks a significant step forward in the investigation. The committee is determined to get to the bottom of the events surrounding the Capitol riot and ensure that justice is served.

In a statement, Representative Thompson emphasized the importance of the investigation, stating, "The American people deserve to know the truth about what happened on January 6. We will not rest until we have uncovered all the facts and held those responsible accountable."

The investigation is ongoing, and the House panel is expected to continue issuing subpoenas and gathering evidence in the coming weeks. The committee's efforts are crucial in ensuring that the truth behind the Capitol riot is revealed and that such events are prevented in the future.
