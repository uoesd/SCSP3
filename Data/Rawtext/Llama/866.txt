Tiger Woods Undergoes Emergency Surgery After Rollover Crash in Los Angeles

Golf legend Tiger Woods is recovering from emergency surgery on his lower right leg and ankle after a rollover crash in Los Angeles early Tuesday morning. The crash occurred in an area known for speeding and crashes, and Woods was the only person in the vehicle, which sustained major damage.

According to Dr. Anish Mahajan, Chief Medical Officer and Interim CEO at Harbor-UCLA Medical Center, Woods suffered significant orthopedic injuries to his right lower extremity. "He is awake and responsive, and we are monitoring his condition closely," Dr. Mahajan said in a statement.

The crash occurred just before 7:00 a.m. local time, and Woods had to be extracted from his vehicle by first responders. Sheriff Alex Villanueva of the Los Angeles County Sheriff's Department said that the crash is being treated as an accident, and no charges are anticipated.

Deputy Carlos Gonzalez of the Los Angeles County Sheriff's Department said that Woods was wearing his seatbelt at the time of the crash, which increased his survival chances. "It's a good thing he was wearing his seatbelt, or it could have been a lot worse," Deputy Gonzalez said.

Woods was in Los Angeles for his annual golf tournament, and had been seen filming with celebrities prior to the crash. This marks Woods' third automobile incident, with previous occurrences in 2009 and 2017.

The crash occurred on Hawthorne Boulevard, a road known for speeding and crashes. According to Sheriff Villanueva, there have been 13 accidents in the area since last January. "It's a dangerous road, and we've had a lot of accidents there," Sheriff Villanueva said.

Woods' agent, Mark Steinberg, released a statement saying, "Tiger Woods was involved in a serious car accident in Los Angeles early this morning. He is currently undergoing surgery and we thank you for your privacy and support."

Woods, 45, is one of the most successful golfers of all time, with 82 PGA Tour victories and 15 major championships. He has been recovering from a back injury and recently announced that he would not be competing in the upcoming Masters tournament.

The golf community has rallied around Woods, with many of his fellow golfers expressing their support and well wishes on social media. "Thinking of @TigerWoods and wishing him a speedy recovery," tweeted Rory McIlroy. "Hope he's doing okay," tweeted Justin Thomas.
