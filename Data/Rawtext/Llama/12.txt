US Capitol Complex Locked Down During Inauguration Rehearsal

Washington, DC - The US Capitol complex was temporarily locked down on Tuesday during a rehearsal for President-elect Joe Biden's inauguration, following a fire in a nearby homeless encampment that raised security concerns.

The acting chief of Capitol Police, Yogananda Pittman, ordered the evacuation and lockdown as a precautionary measure, according to officials. The fire, which was located about 1.6 kilometers away from the Capitol, was extinguished by the fire department, with no injuries reported.

Officials stated that there was no threat to the public or the inauguration from the fire, and the lockdown was purely a precautionary measure. President-elect Joe Biden was not present at the rehearsal during the lockdown.

The incident comes after heightened security measures were put in place following the January 6 riot at the US Capitol, which left several people injured and resulted in the death of a police officer. Security in and around the Capitol has been increased, with the area being on high alert and lockdown.

Participants of the rehearsal, including a military band, were directed to move to a secure location inside the Capitol complex. The US Secret Service announced on Twitter that the lockdown was a precautionary measure, confirming there was no public threat.

The fire department extinguished the fire near the Capitol, confirming there were no injuries and explaining the source of the smoke seen. The incident highlights the increased security concerns in the nation's capital ahead of the inauguration, which is set to take place on January 20.

The lockdown was lifted after a few hours, and the rehearsal resumed. The incident serves as a reminder of the heightened security measures in place, and the efforts being made to ensure the safety of the public and officials during the inauguration.

In a statement, Pittman said, "We take all threats seriously and are taking appropriate measures to ensure the safety of everyone involved in the inauguration."
