House Republicans Retain Liz Cheney, Reject Push to Remove Marjorie Taylor Greene from Committees

In a decisive vote, House Republicans chose to retain Rep. Liz Cheney as their No. 3 leader and rejected a push to remove Rep. Marjorie Taylor Greene from her committees. The vote came as the GOP faces a pivotal moment of direction post-Trump presidency, torn between embracing Trump's divisiveness and returning to traditional conservative values.

Minority Leader Kevin McCarthy criticized Democrats for attempting to remove Greene from her committees, calling it a "partisan power grab." However, McCarthy publicly condemned Greene's endorsement of conspiracy theories and violent views after initially saying little about her.

Rep. Liz Cheney, who voted to impeach Trump, stood by her decision in a closed-door session. "I'm not going to apologize for voting my conscience," she said.

The internal GOP conflicts highlight Trump's continuing influence over the party. Senate Minority Leader Mitch McConnell and other GOP senators have criticized Greene, signaling a division within the party.

Chair Jim McGovern, D-Mass., criticized the GOP's decision to retain Cheney, saying, "It's disappointing, but not surprising, that House Republicans have chosen to double down on their support for Rep. Cheney's extreme views."

The issue of dealing with Greene has put House Republicans under bipartisan pressure, including criticism from McConnell. "The Republican Party has a responsibility to address the toxic rhetoric and conspiracy theories that have become far too prevalent in our politics," he said.

In a 145-61 vote, House Republicans overwhelmingly supported Cheney, rejecting a hard-right conservative push to remove her for her impeachment vote against then-President Donald Trump.

Rep. Marjorie Taylor Greene has shown support on social media for violence against Democrats and various conspiracy theories. Despite this, McCarthy defended her, saying, "We're not going to remove her from her committees. We're not going to take away her ability to serve."

Greene's comments have sparked outrage among Democrats and some Republicans. "It's unacceptable for any member of Congress to promote violence or conspiracy theories," said McGovern.

The GOP's decision to retain Cheney and reject the push to remove Greene from her committees highlights the party's ongoing struggle to define its post-Trump identity.

In a statement, Cheney said, "I'm grateful for the support of my colleagues and I look forward to continuing to work with them to promote the values and principles of our party."

The vote also underscores the ongoing influence of Trump within the GOP. Despite losing the presidency, Trump remains a powerful force in the party, with many members still loyal to him.
