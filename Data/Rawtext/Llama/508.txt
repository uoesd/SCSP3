Governor Hochul and FEMA Announce Major Disaster Declaration for Nassau County

Governor Kathy Hochul and the Federal Emergency Management Agency (FEMA) have announced that Nassau County has been included in the Major Disaster Declaration for Hurricane Ida relief. This declaration extends federal assistance to several New York counties, including both Individual and Public Assistance, to support those recovering from the storm.

"I am grateful to President Biden and FEMA Administrator Deanne Criswell for their support in securing this Major Disaster Declaration," Governor Hochul said. "This declaration will provide crucial funds for housing, counseling, and legal services for those affected by Hurricane Ida. We will continue to work tirelessly to ensure that all New Yorkers have access to the resources they need to recover and rebuild."

Nassau County Executive Laura Curran highlighted her efforts to secure the disaster declaration and urged affected residents to apply for aid. "I want to thank Governor Hochul and FEMA for their support in securing this declaration," Curran said. "I urge all residents who were affected by Hurricane Ida to apply for the available assistance. We are committed to helping our communities recover and rebuild."

The Major Disaster Declaration facilitates financial help for local communities and individual New Yorkers. The New York State Division of Homeland Security and Emergency Services worked closely with FEMA to assess the damage and apply for federal aid. Storm damages to public infrastructure and homes are estimated to be over $140 million, with 2,900 homes affected.

FEMA's Public Assistance Program will support the restoration of public infrastructure, including roads, bridges, and public buildings. In addition, Governor Hochul has ensured the allocation of $378 million in hazard mitigation funding from FEMA to strengthen state infrastructure against extreme weather.

"We are committed to helping New Yorkers recover from Hurricane Ida," said Deanne Criswell, FEMA Administrator. "The Major Disaster Declaration is an important step in providing support to those affected. We will continue to work closely with Governor Hochul and state officials to ensure that all available resources are utilized to support the recovery efforts."

In conclusion, the Major Disaster Declaration for Nassau County is a significant step in supporting the recovery efforts of those affected by Hurricane Ida. With the extension of federal assistance, including both Individual and Public Assistance, New Yorkers can access the resources they need to rebuild and recover. Governor Hochul and FEMA remain committed to supporting the state's recovery efforts and ensuring that all available resources are utilized to support those affected.
