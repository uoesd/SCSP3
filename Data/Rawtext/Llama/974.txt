Election Disinformation Plummets on Social Media After Trump Ban

In the wake of the January 8th ban of President Trump's Twitter account, there has been a significant decline in election disinformation on social media platforms, according to a report by Zignal Labs. The ban, which was part of a broader effort by social media companies to combat the spread of misinformation, has had a profound impact on the ability of Trump and his allies to spread false claims about the election.

"The ban on President Trump's Twitter account has been a game-changer in the fight against election disinformation," said Kate Starbird, a disinformation researcher at the University of Washington. "We've seen a steep decline in the number of false claims being spread on social media, and it's clear that the ban has had a significant impact on the ability of Trump and his allies to reach new audiences."

According to Zignal's report, the volume of disinformation dropped dramatically across multiple platforms, including Twitter, Facebook, Instagram, Snapchat, Twitch, Spotify, and Shopify. The ban also had a significant impact on the ability of Trump's allies to spread false claims, as many of their accounts were also suspended or banned.

Graham Brookie, the director of the Atlantic Council's Digital Forensic Research Lab, noted that the ban was particularly effective in curbing Trump's ability to spread misleading claims about the election. "The ban was a significant blow to Trump's ability to use social media to spread disinformation," Brookie said. "It's clear that the ban has had a profound impact on the ability of Trump and his allies to reach new audiences and spread false claims."

The ban was part of a broader effort by social media companies to combat the spread of misinformation. Twitter also banned over 70,000 QAnon-affiliated accounts, and the use of hashtags affiliated with the Capitol riot also dropped considerably.

"De-platforming can be an effective way to curb the spread of misinformation, as it removes the ability of individuals and groups to reach new audiences and spread false claims," Palen said.

"It's important for social media companies to consider the potential impact of de-platforming and to take steps to mitigate any negative effects."
