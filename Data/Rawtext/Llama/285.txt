Delta Plus: A New Coronavirus Variant Raises Concerns

A new coronavirus variant, known as delta plus, has been detected in several countries, including the US, the UK, Portugal, Russia, China, and South Korea. This mutation of the delta variant has been classified as a "variant of concern" in India due to its potential to bind better to lung cells and possibly evade COVID treatments.

According to Dr. Gagandeep Kang, a virologist, "The 'plus' in delta plus refers to the K417N spike protein mutation, which may make some COVID-19 treatments less effective." However, it is important to note that so far, delta plus has not been shown to be more transmissible or dominant over the original delta variant.

Colin Angus, a public health policy modeler, notes that there's no clear evidence that delta plus has gained a foothold over existing variants. "It's important to keep in mind that the virus is constantly mutating, and new variants will continue to emerge," he said.

Dr. Anthony Fauci, US President Joe Biden's medical advisor, warns that as long as a significant portion of the population remains unvaccinated, the virus will continue to mutate. "The best way to prevent the emergence of new variants is to get as many people vaccinated as possible," he said.

Fauci stresses the importance of widespread vaccination not only for individual protection but for the larger community's safety. "We need to get ahead of the virus and not let it get ahead of us," he said. "The more people who are vaccinated, the less opportunity the virus has to mutate and become resistant to treatments."

Despite the emergence of delta plus, there are currently no "variants of high consequence," according to Fauci. However, the potential exists for future variants to evade vaccines and cause more severe disease. "We must remain vigilant and continue to monitor the situation closely," he said.

Kang emphasizes the need for more research to determine if delta plus poses a greater risk of severe disease. "We need to understand the full implications of this mutation and how it affects the behavior of the virus," she said.

Angus agrees, noting that it's crucial to track the spread of delta plus and monitor its impact on public health. "We need to be prepared to adjust our strategies as needed to ensure that we're doing everything we can to prevent the spread of the virus," he said.

In response to the emergence of delta plus, the World Health Organization (WHO) has urged countries to continue their efforts to combat the virus, including increasing vaccination rates and implementing public health measures such as social distancing and mask-wearing.

The Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) has also issued a statement reminding the public to continue taking precautions to prevent the spread of COVID-19, such as washing hands frequently, avoiding close contact with others, and staying home if symptomatic.
