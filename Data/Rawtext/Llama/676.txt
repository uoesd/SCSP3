However, it also comes with risks of cross-examination, which prosecutors may exploit to look for inconsistencies or admissions. Defendants who choose not to testify may also be seen as having something to hide.

Elizabeth Holmes, who was accused of fraud in her role as CEO of Theranos, took the stand and denied any wrongdoing. Her testimony was seen as a risky move, as prosecutors were able to highlight inconsistencies in her story. She was ultimately convicted.

The trend of defendants taking the stand may continue to grow, as more people become comfortable with the idea of sharing their story publicly. However, it's important to note that testifying is a risky strategy that should be carefully considered by defendants and their lawyers.
