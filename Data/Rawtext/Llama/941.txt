The Senate's Failure to Remove Trump Has Emboldened His Assault on Democracy

The House of Representatives impeached President Donald Trump in December 2019 for abuse of power and obstruction of Congress, stemming from a scheme involving Ukraine. However, the Republican-led Senate voted not to remove him from office in February, shortly before the COVID-19 pandemic became a global crisis. Trump's recent actions, particularly his attempt to pressure Brad Raffensperger of Georgia to overturn the state's election results, mirror his previous behavior with Ukraine and demonstrate a consistent pattern of attempting to manipulate electoral outcomes.

David A. Graham, a staff writer at The Atlantic, suggests that Trump's behavior has been consistent and reflects a pattern of attempting to manipulate electoral outcomes. Trump's actions are viewed as a direct consequence of the Senate's failure to remove him from office, leading to attempts to undermine the 2020 election results. Legal experts are unsure if Trump could face criminal liability for his actions concerning Georgia's election results. However, the impeachment process was seen as the appropriate mechanism for holding a president accountable, rather than the criminal justice system.

Only one Republican senator, Mitt Romney, voted to convict Trump in the impeachment trial. The Republican response to Trump's behaviors and claims continues to be largely supportive or evasive. This lack of consequences for Trump's actions is seen as damaging to democracy, setting a precedent for future political misconduct and eroding trust in the electoral system.

The fact that Trump feels emboldened to continue his assault on democracy is a direct result of the Senate's failure to hold him accountable. His actions are not only a threat to the integrity of the electoral process but also a violation of the public's trust. The Republican Party's continued support for Trump, despite his blatant disregard for the rule of law, is a betrayal of their duty to protect and defend the Constitution.

The damage that Trump has inflicted on our democracy is not limited to his time in office. His actions have set a dangerous precedent for future presidents, who may feel emboldened to engage in similar behavior. The erosion of trust in the electoral system will have long-lasting consequences, potentially leading to a decline in voter participation and a loss of faith in the democratic process.
