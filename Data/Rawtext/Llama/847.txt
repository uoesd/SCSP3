Tiger Woods' Recovery Going Well, Says Close Friend Justin Thomas

In a recent interview on the No Laying Up podcast, Justin Thomas provided an update on Tiger Woods' recovery, stating that he visits Woods two to three times a week and that his recovery is going well. Thomas, a close friend of Woods and fellow PGA Tour pro, also noted that Woods can participate in his children's activities, indicating some level of improvement.

Thomas' comments offer a rare glimpse into Woods' private life, as the golf legend has maintained a low public profile since his car accident in Los Angeles nine months ago. While details about Woods' recovery have been scarce, occasional updates from fellow golfers like Rory McIlroy and Rickie Fowler have suggested that Woods is making progress.

According to Thomas, Woods' personality remains unchanged, retaining his characteristic sense of humor despite the challenges he faces. However, there is still uncertainty regarding Woods' future in professional golf. Thomas believes that Woods will attempt a comeback if possible, but emphasizes that Woods prioritizes being a father over his golfing career.

Thomas' interview also covered topics beyond Woods, including his own season, mental health, and views on the state of golf. When asked about his own game, Thomas expressed satisfaction with his recent performances, citing his win at the BMW Championship as a highlight.

Regarding mental health, Thomas stressed the importance of self-care and acknowledged the challenges that professional golfers face in maintaining their mental well-being. He emphasized the need for golfers to prioritize their mental health, citing examples of players who have struggled with depression and anxiety.

On the topic of the state of golf, Thomas expressed optimism about the sport's future, highlighting the emergence of young talent and the growing popularity of golf among younger generations. He also praised the efforts of organizations like the PGA Tour and the LPGA to promote golf and make it more accessible to a wider audience.

Thomas' interview offered valuable insights into the world of professional golf, as well as a rare glimpse into the private life of Tiger Woods.
