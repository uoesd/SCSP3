He added, "Absent gas deliverability and cost issues, Uri would have had a slightly positive impact on 2021 EBITDA."

As a result of the storm, Vistra has revised its 2021 EBITDA and ongoing operations adjusted free cash flow guidance. The revised EBITDA range is $1,475-$1,875 million, and the free cash flow range is $200-$600 million. Before the storm, Vistra had estimated significantly higher EBITDA and free cash flow for 2021.

Despite the increased costs, Vistra will not buy additional shares in 2021 but will maintain its quarterly dividend. In a statement, Morgan said, "We remain committed to our dividend and will continue to evaluate opportunities to return capital to shareholders."
