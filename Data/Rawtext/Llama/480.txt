The couple started their handcuffed journey on Valentine's Day, hoping to strengthen their bond and overcome their relationship struggles. They experienced every daily activity together, from showering and eating to working and sleeping, and shared their experiences with their growing social media following.

Pustovitova expressed her emotional struggle during the breakup, reflecting on the lesson learned from their experience. "It was a difficult decision to make, but we realized that we were not meant to be together," she said.

Despite their separation, the couple decided to auction off the handcuffs they used during their experiment, with part of the proceeds going to charity. The auction was attended by Ukrainian TV news channels and a record book representative, solidifying their place in history as the couple who remained handcuffed together for the longest period.

The couple's final Instagram post depicted them standing apart, signaling the end of their unique journey together. "We are grateful for the support and attention we received during our experiment, but we have decided to move on and focus on our individual lives," said Kudlay.

Their separation was made official in a public event, marking the end of their 123-day handcuffed adventure. "We hope that our story will inspire others to take risks and try new things, even if it means stepping out of their comfort zone," said Pustovitova.

The couple's decision to auction off the handcuffs and donate part of the proceeds to charity is a testament to their commitment to giving back to their community. "We are grateful for the opportunity to make a difference and hope that our contribution will inspire others to do the same," said Kudlay.

In the end, Pustovitova and Kudlay's handcuffed experiment may not have saved their relationship, but it has left a lasting impact on their lives and the lives of those around them.
