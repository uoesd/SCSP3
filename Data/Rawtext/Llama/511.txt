Governor Parson Mobilizes Missouri National Guard to Aid in Hurricane Ida Recovery Efforts

Governor Mike Parson of Missouri has authorized the mobilization of approximately 300 Soldiers from the Missouri National Guard to assist in Hurricane Ida recovery efforts in Louisiana. This deployment is in response to a request from Louisiana through the Emergency Management Assistance Compact (EMAC), a mutual aid agreement among states for sharing resources during emergencies.

Governor Parson emphasized the importance of states helping each other in times of crisis, and expressed confidence in the skills of the Missouri National Guard in recovery efforts. "We are proud to be able to assist our neighbors in Louisiana during this difficult time," Governor Parson said. "The Missouri National Guard has a proven track record of providing critical support in times of crisis, and we are eager to help in any way we can."

The Missouri National Guard will provide humanitarian support, equipment, debris removal, and general support to affected areas in Louisiana. Soldiers from the 110th Maneuver Enhancement Brigade and the 35th Engineer Brigade are among those being mobilized. According to Maj. Gen. Levon Cumpton, Missouri National Guard Adjutant General, "The Missouri National Guard has a wealth of experience in recovery operations, and we are ready to assist our fellow Americans in their time of need."

The deployment is set to last up to 14 days, with soldiers ready to assist as long as needed. The effort reflects a broader spirit of Americans helping Americans in times of crisis, and is part of a mutual aid agreement among states for sharing resources during emergencies.

In conclusion, the mobilization of the Missouri National Guard to aid in Hurricane Ida recovery efforts is a testament to the spirit of cooperation and mutual aid that exists among states in times of crisis. As Governor Parson noted, "We are proud to be able to assist our neighbors in Louisiana during this difficult time, and we will do everything we can to help them recover and rebuild."
