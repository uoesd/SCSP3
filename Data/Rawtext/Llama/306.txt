Ever Given Ship Finally Freed from Suez Canal Banks After Six-Day Ordeal

The Ever Given container ship, which had been stuck sideways in the Suez Canal for six days, was finally freed on Monday after a complex and challenging salvage operation. The ship, which measures 1,312 feet in length, was successfully refloated using a combination of powerful tugboats, large-capacity dredgers, and high tides.

The salvage effort involved removing 30,000 cubic meters of sand, equivalent to about a dozen Olympic-sized swimming pools, from the canal banks. The Suez Canal Authority chairman estimated the daily disruption cost of the canal's blockage at $15 billion.

The refloating operation was a collaborative effort between conventional earthmoving equipment and specialized ships like dredgers and tugboats. Two dredgers, the 10th of Ramadan and the Mashhour, played a significant role in the operation, along with 14 tugboats that benefited from high tides caused by a full moon.

The operation was not without its challenges, as the ship's size and the narrowness of the canal made it difficult to maneuver. However, the salvage team was able to successfully refloat the ship, allowing it to continue its journey through the canal.

The blockage of the Suez Canal had caused a significant backlog of ships waiting to pass through, with estimates suggesting it could take anywhere from three to more than ten days to clear the backlog. Over 300 ships were waiting to pass through the canal, adding to the congestion.

In addition, over 130 additional vessels were en route to the Suez Canal, further complicating the situation. However, with the successful refloating of the Ever Given, officials are hopeful that the backlog can be cleared quickly and the canal can return to normal operations.

The incident has highlighted the importance of the Suez Canal, which is a vital shipping route that connects Europe and Asia. The canal is a significant source of revenue for Egypt, and any disruption to its operations can have far-reaching consequences for global trade.

The successful refloating of the Ever Given is a testament to the ingenuity and expertise of the salvage team, who worked tirelessly to resolve the situation as quickly and safely as possible.
