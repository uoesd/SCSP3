Tiger Woods Involved in High-Speed Crash in Rolling Hills Estates

Golf legend Tiger Woods was involved in a serious car crash in Rolling Hills Estates earlier this year, leaving him with a broken leg. The crash occurred on a notorious stretch of Hawthorne Boulevard known for bad accidents, and according to Los Angeles County sheriff's officials, it was caused by Woods driving at nearly twice the speed limit and failing to handle a curve.

Sheriff Alex Villanueva stated that Woods' vehicle hit a median, crossed into an opposing lane, hit a tree, and then rolled over. Data from the vehicle's black box showed Woods was traveling 75 mph upon hitting the tree. Woods did not hit the brakes before the collision, possibly hitting the accelerator inadvertently, and does not recall the crash.

Woods was trapped in the vehicle until rescued by firefighters and paramedics. Villanueva said Woods showed no signs of impairment and was not charged with a crime or cited for an infraction.

The crash occurred while Woods was in Los Angeles County for a two-day video shoot. He was driving a courtesy vehicle at the time of the crash.

In a statement, Woods acknowledged the sheriff's investigation and thanked the responders, focusing on his recovery and family.

Capt. James Powers of the Los Angeles County Sheriff's Department said, "We're grateful that Mr. Woods was not more seriously injured, and we wish him a speedy recovery."

The stretch of Hawthorne Boulevard where the crash occurred has been the site of numerous accidents in the past, leading locals to dub it "Blood Alley."
