Vice President Harris Announces U.S.-France Collaboration on Space and Cybersecurity

Washington, D.C. - Vice President Kamala Harris today announced a series of collaborative initiatives between the United States and France on space and cybersecurity, following her meeting with French President Emmanuel Macron. These initiatives highlight the U.S. commitment to working with allies on 21st-century challenges and demonstrate the strength of the U.S.-France partnership.

In the area of space, the United States and France will enhance their cooperation in the civil, commercial, and national security sectors. The two countries aim to address global issues such as climate crisis, space exploration, STEM education, space environment sustainability, and space economy. To achieve these goals, the U.S. and France will establish a comprehensive dialogue on space, with participation from various departments and agencies on both sides. Additionally, the United States has committed to joining the Space Climate Observatory (SCO), which will make space data accessible for climate crisis mitigation and response.

In the area of cybersecurity, Vice President Harris announced U.S. support for the Paris Call for Trust and Security in Cyberspace, which aligns with the Biden-Harris Administration's priority on international cybersecurity engagement. The U.S. aims to improve cybersecurity through various international partnerships, including actions against cybercrime and updates to NATO cyber policy. The support for the Paris Call demonstrates the U.S. commitment to upholding global norms in cyberspace and working with allies to address cyber threats.

The initiatives announced today demonstrate the strong partnership between the United States and France and the shared commitment to addressing global challenges. The U.S. looks forward to continuing to work closely with France and other allies to promote peace, security, and prosperity in the 21st century.
