Hannity's Show Reigns Supreme: Fox News Dominates Cable News Ratings

Sean Hannity's show on Fox News Channel was the most-watched cable news show last week, solidifying the network's position as the leader in cable news. According to Nielsen Media Research, Hannity's show attracted 3.7 million viewers, slightly ahead of Tucker Carlson Tonight, which drew 3.6 million viewers. Hannity also led in the 25-54 age demographic, with 669,000 viewers, while Tucker Carlson Tonight was a close second with 654,000 viewers.

Fox News Channel had an average total audience of 2.980 million viewers for the week of August 16 through 22, the highest among cable news networks. The network outperformed not only CNN and MSNBC but also the broadcast networks in prime time. Other top shows on Fox included The Five, The Ingraham Angle, and Special Report with Bret Baier.

Chris Cuomo's return to CNN's Cuomo Prime Time resulted in a total audience of 1.076 million viewers, a 28% decline from the previous week. The show also saw a reduction of more than 30% in viewership in the 25-54 age demographic. CNN experienced a significant drop in women viewers for Cuomo Prime Time, down 30% for the week.

Fox News Channel's dominance in cable news ratings is a testament to the network's commitment to providing high-quality programming that resonates with viewers. With a strong lineup of shows and a focus on delivering accurate and unbiased reporting, Fox News Channel is well-positioned to continue its reign as the leader in cable news.
