Navy Corpsman Maxton W. Soviak Posthumously Promoted, Awarded Purple Heart

WASHINGTON â€“ In a ceremony held at the Pentagon, Navy Hospital Corpsman Maxton W. Soviak was posthumously promoted to petty officer 3rd class and awarded the Purple Heart and Fleet Marine Force Corpsman warfare badge. Soviak was killed in an Islamic State attack at Kabul airport during the U.S. evacuation mission on Aug. 26, 2021.

Soviak, a native of Cleveland, Ohio, was assigned to the 1st Marine Regiment, 1st Marine Division, Camp Pendleton, Calif. He was among 13 U.S. service members who lost their lives in the attack, which occurred during the events leading up to the end of the U.S. mission in Afghanistan.

Navy Secretary Carlos Del Toro recognized Soviak's dedication and service to the nation, stating, "Hospital Corpsman Soviak's sacrifice will never be forgotten. His bravery and commitment to his fellow service members and the Afghan people are a testament to the very best of our Navy's values."

The attack on Kabul airport was part of a series of events that led to the end of the U.S. mission in Afghanistan. U.S. and coalition forces evacuated over 124,000 people from Afghanistan starting in late July.

Soviak's remains were transferred at Dover Air Force Base and will arrive at Cleveland Hopkins International Airport. A public procession for Soviak is planned to follow the arrival of his remains.

The promotion and awards ceremony was attended by Soviak's family, friends, and fellow service members. His promotion to petty officer 3rd class was effective as of the date of his death.

Soviak's awards and decorations include the Purple Heart, Fleet Marine Force Corpsman warfare badge, National Defense Service Medal, and Afghanistan Campaign Medal.

In a statement, Soviak's family expressed their pride in his service and sacrifice, saying, "Maxton was a dedicated sailor and a loving son, brother, and friend. He will always be remembered for his bravery and selflessness."

The loss of Hospital Corpsman Maxton W. Soviak is a reminder of the sacrifices made by U.S. service members in the line of duty. His posthumous promotion and awards are a testament to his dedication and service to the nation.
