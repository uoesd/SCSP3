One form of relief that has been provided to families is the expanded federal Child Tax Credit, which was included in the American Rescue Plan. This provision provides direct payments to families, with $300 for each child under 6 and $250 for each child ages 6 to 17, between July and December of 2021. A family with one child under 6 will receive $1,800 by December 2021 through the advanced payments.

In addition, families not normally filing a tax return could still benefit if they notified the IRS by November 15. These eligible families will receive half of their total Child Tax Credit in a single payment on December 15. The potential one-time payments are up to $1,800 for each child under 6, and up to $1,500 for each child ages 6 to 17.

The future of the expanded Child Tax Credit is uncertain, with the Biden administration aiming to make it permanent. However, Democratic Senator Joe Manchin has expressed a desire to make changes to the Child Tax Credit payments.
