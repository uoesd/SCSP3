Prince William is taking a page out of his brother's book, embracing a more eco-friendly approach for his climate change initiative, the Earthshot Prize. The move marks a shift towards more controversial issues for the royal, who has previously focused on more traditional charitable causes.

The Earthshot Prize, dubbed the eco-friendly Oscars, aims to promote solutions to climate change over the next decade. In his speech, William criticized space tourism, urging figures like Jeff Bezos and Elon Musk to invest in saving the planet instead. "We need some of the world's greatest brains and minds fixed on trying to repair this planet, not trying to find the next place to go and live," he said.

The event and William's statements haven't faced the same level of criticism as similar efforts by Meghan Markle, indicating a nuanced public reception. While some have praised William's commitment to environmental issues, others have criticized the royal family's carbon footprint.

Despite the mixed reactions, Prince William remains committed to his mission. "The Earthshot Prize is about celebrating the people and projects that are working towards a better future for our planet," he said. "We need to take action now, and we need to work together to make a difference."
