Massive Container Ship Successfully Freed from Suez Canal After Six-Day Ordeal

In a monumental effort that captured the attention of the global shipping industry, the Ever Given, a massive container ship, has been successfully freed from the Suez Canal after being stuck for six days. The operation required a combination of dredging, tugboat maneuvering, and taking advantage of high tides, and was hailed as a significant relief for global trade.

The Ever Given, which measures 400 meters in length and 59 meters in width, became wedged in the canal on March 23 due to poor visibility and high winds. The incident prompted a global effort to dislodge the ship, with experts likening its situation to "an enormous beached whale."

Gregory Tylawsky, a licensed shipmaster and founder of the Maritime Expert Group, described the challenge as "unprecedented." "We've never seen a ship this size get stuck in the Suez Canal," he said.

To free the ship, dredgers and more than 10 tugboats worked round the clock, removing sand, mud, and water from around the vessel. The 10th of Ramadan and the Mashhour, two heavy-duty dredgers, played significant roles in the dredging process.

Peter Berdowski, CEO of Dutch dredging company Boskalis, said, "It was a complex operation that required careful planning and execution. We had to carefully assess the situation and come up with a plan that would allow us to safely and effectively dislodge the ship."

To aid in refloating the ship, 9,000 tons of the Ever Given's ballast water were released. The ship was fully dislodged on March 29, following efforts that coincided with high tide conditions due to a full moon.

Ragui Assaad, a professor at the University of Minnesota and research fellow at the Economic Research Forum in Cairo, noted the significance of the Suez Canal for global trade. "The Suez Canal is a crucial waterway for global trade, with about 15% of all shipping passing through it. The blockage caused by the Ever Given had significant implications for global supply chains."
