Texas Man Arrested for Role in US Capitol Riots, Faces 12 Counts

A Texas man, Garret Miller, has been arrested and indicted on 12 counts related to his involvement in the US Capitol riots on January 6, 2021. Miller, who was wearing a T-shirt that read "Take America Back" and "I Was There, Washington D.C., January 6, 2021" at the time of his arrest, is accused of wanting to disrupt the democratic process by bringing tactical gear, ropes, and a gun to the Capitol.

According to prosecutors, Miller documented his actions during the Capitol siege on social media, admitting to entering the Capitol building. He also made threatening statements towards Rep. Alexandria Ocasio-Cortez and a Capitol police officer on social media. Prosecutors are requesting that Miller remain jailed while awaiting trial, citing the incriminating nature of his T-shirt and social media posts.

Miller faced a federal magistrate judge in Texas and has been ordered to remain detained after his January 20 arrest. On February 12, a grand jury indicted Miller on 12 counts, including civil disorder and assaulting officers.

F. Clinton Broden, Miller's defense attorney, has requested his release, arguing that he has no history of violence and expressed regret for his actions. However, prosecutors maintain that Miller poses a danger to the community and should remain in custody.

Miller remains in jail in Oklahoma City after an injury, delaying his transport to Washington for trial. His trial date has not yet been set.

The riots were sparked by a dispute over the results of the 2020 presidential election, with then-President Donald Trump and his supporters claiming without evidence that the election was rigged against him.
