Lawrence Hometown Hero: The Legacy of Marine Sgt. Johanny Rosario

A City and a Nation Mourn the Loss of a Valiant Marine

In a somber yet proud reflection on sacrifice and service, the community of Lawrence, Massachusetts, and the nation as a whole are honoring the valor of Marine Sgt. Johanny Rosario Pichardo, 25, who was tragically killed in a suicide bombing at the Kabul airport amidst a chaotic evacuation effort. Rosario, alongside Sgt. Nicole L. Gee and 11 other U.S. service members, was a beacon of hope and courage, screening women and children in Afghanistan until the very end. Their ultimate sacrifice did not go unackreciated, echoing through the hearts of many as they grieve the loss.

First Lieutenant John "Jack" Coppola of the Marine Corps remembered Rosario's dedication with heartfelt words, saying, "Sgt. Rosario's service and sacrifice are the very embodiment of what it means to be a Marine. Her bravery, in the face of danger, stands as a testament to the finest qualities of our nation's call to service."

The city of Lawrence, where Rosario grew up, plans to pay tribute to her heroism and unwavering spirit in a manner befitting such a distinguished servicewoman. Mayor Kendrys Vasquez confirmed this commitment, stating, "Our city is heartbroken but immensely proud. We will honor the legacy of Sgt. Johanny Rosario in a way that commemorates her valiant service and the profound sacrifice she made for our country."

Throughout her 6 1/2 years in the military, Rosario garnered multiple commendations, reflecting her outstanding commitment and dedication to her role. These accolades were a mere glimpse into her character, which inspired many within and outside the military.

Retired U.S. Army Major Kathleen Romano praised Rosario for her leadership, saying, "Sgt. Rosario led by example, demonstrating what it means to uphold the highest standards of the military, all while carrying herself with unmistakable grace and humility."

Reverend Paul O'Brien of St. Patrick Parish in Lawrence also spoke highly of her, noting, "Johanny's spirit was one of compassion and resolve. In her, we saw the very best of what our community and our nation represent."

The loss has reverberated not only through the community but also at the international level, with the Dominican Republic's embassy expressing its condolences. She was a beacon of hope and pride for our community, and her sacrifice will never be forgotten."

U.S. Senators Elizabeth Warren and Edward J. Markey paid their respects as well, recognizing Rosario's bravery and the pain of such a loss. Senator Warren stated, "Her courage in the face of danger exemplifies the best of our military tradition. We mourn her loss deeply but are forever grateful for her sacrifice."

Senator Markey echoed these sentiments, emphasizing, "Sgt. Rosario's service was a profound act of bravery and dedication. Her legacy is one of valor and unwavering commitment to her country and her fellow service members."

Before enlisting, Rosario briefly attended Bridgewater State University, studying criminal justice - a testament to her lifelong commitment to serving and protecting others. Frederick W. Clark Jr., President of Bridgewater State University, remembered her as "an exceptional student who brought determination and a strong sense of integrity to everything she did."

In Boston's Seaport District, a vigil led by Massachusetts Fallen Heroes brought together community members to honor Rosario and the other service members killed in the attack. This solemn gathering underscored the collective mourning and respect felt across the state.

Jaime Melendez Jr., Director of Veterans' Services in Lawrence, expressed the city's grief, underscoring the magnitude of the loss: "Every soldier's death is a stark reminder of the costs of war. Sgt. Rosario's sacrifice leaves a void in our hearts, but her legacy will forever be a part of our community."

Wanda Marquez, a program worker at Salem State University's Upward Bound Program, where Rosario was a mentor, shared her deep sorrow: "Johanny was more than a mentor; she was a role model and a beacon of light for so many young people. Her loss is immeasurable."

The community's grief is a poignant reminder of the harsh realities faced by those who serve. An Air Force veteran, reflecting the sentiments of many, shared their frustration and sorrow, saying, "It's a tragic loss for all of us. Johanny embodied the best of what it means to serve one's country. She will be profoundly missed."

As the city of Lawrence and the nation mourn, they also stand in unwavering gratitude to Sgt. Johanny Rosario Pichardo. Her sacrifice, along with those who fell beside her, will remain etched in memory, serving as a lasting symbol of courage, service, and the ultimate price of freedom. In honoring her legacy, we reaffirm our commitment to remembering those who have made the ultimate sacrifice and recognizing the burden carried by military families, friends, and communities across the country.
