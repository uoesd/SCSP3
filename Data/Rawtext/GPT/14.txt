These precautions came in the wake of a violent attack by rioters loyal to former President Donald Trump, raising concerns of potential disturbances on the day of the swearing-in.

The federal and local law enforcement agencies had heightened their vigilance, closely monitoring far-right extremist and militia groups for possible threats leading up to and on Inauguration Day. Despite these concerns and the massive security presence, the day passed with only a few scattered arrests and no reported major protests or disruptions.

The security operation for the inauguration was monumental. Over 25,000 troops and police were deployed to safeguard the event, a response to the breach of the Capitol Building on January 6. The National Mall was closed to the public; its usual throngs of onlookers replaced by an extensive network of checkpoints and barricades.

Federal agents were on high alert, informed by a stream of concerning online chatter and on the lookout for possible attempts by individuals or groups to infiltrate the security perimeter. The atmosphere was tense, with Washington, D.C., transformed into a fortress to ensure the peaceful transfer of power.

Among those disappointed by the day's peace were followers of the QAnon conspiracy theory, who had circulated baseless claims of a dramatic climax to their narratives on Inauguration Day. Instead, their forums and chat groups were filled with expressions of disillusionment as the reality of Biden's presidency settled in.

In an extraordinary step underscoring the gravity of the security fears, twelve National Guard members were removed from their inauguration duties following the identification of extremist statements or ties to right-wing militias. This action highlighted the level of scrutiny and precaution adopted to safeguard the ceremony.

In explaining the measures taken, Gen. Daniel Hokanson, chief of the National Guard Bureau, noted, "Due to the rigorous background checks and the extreme vetting process for all 25,000 National Guard members assigned to the inauguration security, we identified a small number of individuals who made inappropriate comments or had associations that do not align with the values of the National Guard."

This vetting process by the FBI of all National Guard members deployed to the capital was an unprecedented security effort, aimed at ensuring the force's integrity and the safety of the event they were tasked to protect.

The successful and peaceful turnover of power came as a relief to many Americans and observers around the world, especially after the recent tumultuous weeks that saw a direct attack on the democratic process. The authorities have made it clear that while the inauguration day passed without significant incident, security in Washington, D.C., and at state capitol buildings across the country will remain heightened for several days to come.

Officials have commended the comprehensive security measures, the cooperation between agencies, and the peaceful conduct of citizens during the inauguration. This coordination and restraint have underscored the nation's commitment to upholding its democratic traditions, despite the challenges it faces.

As President Biden begins his term, the focus shifts to healing and uniting a fractured country. Nevertheless, the shadow of the past weeks' events and the security concerns they have raised will likely continue to influence the political and social landscape of the United States for some time.

In the end, the inauguration of Joe Biden as the 46th president of the United States marked not only a change in leadership but also a significant test of the country's democratic institutions and their resilience in the face of internal threats. With the ordeal concluded safely, attention now turns to the path forward and the work that lies ahead to rebuild and reconcile.
