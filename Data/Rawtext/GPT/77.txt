Capitol Police Officer Brian Sicknick Memorialized in U.S. Capitol Rotunda

In a solemn ceremony held in the U.S. Capitol Rotunda, Capitol Police Officer Brian Sicknick, who tragically lost his life from injuries sustained during the January 6 Capitol riot, was memorialized by congressional leaders and the Sicknick family. Officer Sicknick's sacrifice was commemorated in a poignant service attended by leading figures from both sides of the political aisle, showcasing the far-reaching impact of his heroism.

House Speaker Nancy Pelosi and Senate Majority Leader Chuck Schumer were among the congressional leaders who paid tribute to Officer Sicknick's bravery and dedication to duty. During the ceremony, Pelosi hailed Sicknick as a true patriot, whose ultimate sacrifice in the line of duty will forever be etched in the nation's memory. "We will never forget the sacrifice and bravery of Officer Brian Sicknick. His service to our country and his courageous efforts to defend our Capitol will always be remembered," Pelosi stated.

Schumer also shared heartfelt words, emphasizing Sicknick's humane and kind nature, and the profound personal loss felt by many. "Brian Sicknick was a good, kind man, whose heroic action on January 6th exemplifies the best of our nation's values. We mourn as a nation for the loss of one of our devoted protectors," Schumer remarked.

The ceremony was attended by members of Congress and Officer Sicknick's grieving family, highlighting the unity in honoring his memory. The previous night, President Joe Biden and First Lady Jill Biden paid their respects to Officer Sicknick, further underscoring the gravity of his sacrifice.

A viewing session was held to allow Capitol Police officers and others to pay their respects, followed by plans for Officer Sicknick's remains to be laid to rest at Arlington National Cemetery. This final resting place is a testament to his brave service to the United States.

Throughout the memorial, Pelosi and Schumer underscored Sicknick's role in defending democracy on January 6, hailing his actions as heroic. Officer Sicknick's legacy is now permanently honored, as he becomes only the fifth person to lie in honor in the Capitol Rotunda, a distinction that speaks volumes of his service and sacrifice.
