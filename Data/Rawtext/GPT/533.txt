Despite the barbaric attack, the Taliban, now controlling the Afghan capital, denounced the blasts and distanced themselves, with spokesman Zabihullah Mujahid conveying their condemnation and emphasizing their non-involvement. As news of the attack spread, countries renewed their cautions against approaching Kabul airport, citing severe security threats. Pentagon spokesman John Kirby, alongside Gen. Acting U.S. ambassador to Kabul, Ross Wilson, reiterated the security risks, urging Americans and allies to heed advice against traveling to the airport.

The ruthless airport assault, unraveling against the backdrop of the impending U.S. withdrawal deadline of August 31, underscores the volatile landscape in Afghanistan, raising profound questions about the future security and stability of the country in the aftermath of the foreign troops' departure.
