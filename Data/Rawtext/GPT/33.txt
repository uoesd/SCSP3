Grambling State University Band to Perform at Presidential Inauguration Festivities

GRAMBLING, La. (KNOE) â€“ In an unprecedented move that celebrates the vibrancy and talent of historically Black colleges and universities (HBCUs), Grambling State University's World Famed Tiger Marching Band is set to captivate the nation during President-elect Joe Biden and Vice-President-elect Kamala Harris's inauguration festivities. Their performance will be a part of 'We Are One,' a special virtual event added to the packed inaugural schedule.

GSU President Rick Gallot expressed immense pride in the band's participation, highlighting its significance against the backdrop of Harris's historic vice presidency. "It's a moment of great pride for all of us at GSU and HBCUs nationwide," Gallot said. "Having the first HBCU graduate as Vice President and our band playing at the inauguration is a powerful testament to our enduring relevance and talent."

Adding to the excitement, Dr. Nikole Roebuck, the esteemed Director of Bands at GSU, shared the honor felt by the band's invitation. "To be recognized and included in such a significant event is a tremendous honor for our students, faculty, and the entire Grambling community. We are thrilled to represent our university and HBCUs on this national stage," Dr. Roebuck stated.

The Tiger Marching Band is no stranger to presidential inaugurations, having previously performed for George W. Bush, Barack Obama, and even played alongside Bill Clinton during his visit to GSU. Their upcoming performance further cements their legacy in providing inspirational music at critical moments in the nation's history.

Scheduled for January 19, 2021, at 7:00 p.m. CST, 'We Are One' will feature the Tiger Marching Band in a thrilling Battle of the Bands segment among other prestigious groups from various universities. Fans and viewers can look forward to a showcase of talent and unity through music.

The event promises widespread accessibility, with broadcasts available on multiple platforms including Urban One, BET, NBC Peacock TV, and others. As the nation approaches the formal swearing-in of Biden and Harris on January 20, this pre-inauguration celebration aims to unify and uplift viewers across the country.

For more information on how to watch the inaugural events and catch the World Famed Tiger Marching Band in action, visit the official inauguration website.
