The Santa Fe County Sheriff's Office has been delving into the details of the day's events, identifying Hannah Gutierrez-Reed, the film's armorer, and Dave Halls, the first assistant director, as key figures who handled the gun prior to the shooting. No charges have been filed as of yet concerning the shooting, but the case remains open to various possibilities. Santa Fe District Attorney Mary Carmack-Altwies stated, "Everything is on the table at this point. We do not know yet whether charges will be filed."

The investigation has broadened its scope to include the origins of the live rounds that ended up on set. A recent search at a local gun store sought to bring new insights into how live ammunition found its way to the 'Rust' set, but officials have yet to confirm the source of the rounds.

In a testament to the deep dive into the incident, the Santa Fe County Sheriff Adan Mendoza referred to the situation as "complex," implying the intricacies involved in piecing together the events of that fateful day. "We're working to gather as much information as possible to determine how this tragedy occurred," Mendoza said.

Adding to the discourse on the incident, two ABC specials are scheduled to air, aiming to shed light on the 'Rust' shooting. These specials will feature interviews with Alec Baldwin among others closely involved, hoping to provide a clearer picture of the sequence of events.

According to a search warrant affidavit, moments before the shooting, Halls handed the gun to Baldwin, communicating to the crew that it was a "cold gun," indicating it should not have contained any live rounds. However, the discharge that followed contradicted this assurance.

Director Joel Souza, who was injured in the incident, has been relatively private, only speaking through official statements. The focus remains on understanding what led to the live ammunition being present in the firearm and how future incidents of this nature can be prevented.

The production of 'Rust' has been halted indefinitely, with cast and crew, along with the broader film industry, reflecting on the protocols concerning firearm safety on set. This incident has spurred calls for stricter control measures and possibly the implementation of new safety standards within the industry.
