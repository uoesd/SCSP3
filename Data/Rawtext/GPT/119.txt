The report detailed allegations that Cuomo sexually harassed 11 women, leading to a cascade of criticism from within his own party. Cuomo's resignation will take effect in two weeks, paving the way for Lt. Gov. Kathy Hochul to become New York's first female governor.

During a televised address, Cuomo voiced his decision, stating, "Given the circumstances, the best way I can help now is if I step aside and let government get back to governing." This announcement comes after the attorney general's investigation found that Cuomo had engaged in conduct constituting sexual harassment under federal and state law.

Cuomo has steadfastly denied any inappropriate behavior, though he acknowledged that some of his actions may have made others uncomfortable. "In my mind, I've never crossed the line with anyone. But, I didn't realize the extent to which the line has been redrawn," Cuomo said. He described his decision to resign not as an admission of guilt but as a sacrifice for the greater good of New York.

Rita Glavin, Cuomo's personal lawyer, launched a vehement defense of him just before his resignation, accusing the attorney general's report of being biased. "The report was the result of a biased investigation that omitted evidence which contradicts the narrative that has been sold to the public," Glavin stated.

State Attorney General Letitia James, however, stood by the findings of her report and deemed Cuomo's resignation a significant step towards accountability. "Today closes a sad chapter for all of New York, but it's an important step towards justice," James said. "I thank the brave women who came forward and spoke their truth."

The pressure on Cuomo had intensified in recent days, with leading state and national Democrats calling for his resignation. Among them was Democratic state Senate Majority Leader Andrea Stewart-Cousins, who expressed her vindication in the thoroughness and outcome of the AG's investigation. "The attorney general's detailed report reveals unacceptable behavior by a governor sworn to serve and protect the citizens of our state. Sexual harassment is not to be tolerated, and accountability must be ensured," said Stewart-Cousins.

Echoing this sentiment, New York State Democratic Chairman Jay Jacobs highlighted the significance of Hochul's forthcoming governorship. "While today's announcement by Governor Cuomo is a sad day for New York State, it also marks a historic moment with Lt. Gov. Kathy Hochul becoming New York's first female governor. She has my complete support," Jacobs declared.

The political fallout from Cuomo's resignation has been widespread, with reactions spanning the spectrum from relief to calls for further action. Sochie Nnaemeka, director of the New York Working Families Party, lauded Cuomo's departure but emphasized the need for continued vigilance. "Cuomo's resignation is a testament to the power of survivor voices and the tireless work of advocates. Our work is not done. The impeachment proceedings must continue to ensure he can no longer wield power in New York politics," Nnaemeka urged.

As New York stands at the threshold of this unprecedented transition, the focus turns to Lt. Gov. Kathy Hochul, who is set to break the glass ceiling as the state's first female governor. Hochul, who has maintained a significantly lower profile than Cuomo, will soon take the reins of a state still grappling with the pandemic and its socio-economic fallout.

Cuomo's resignation marks the conclusion of a political dynasty, with his father Mario Cuomo having served three terms as Governor of New York. As the state looks to move beyond this tumultuous chapter, questions linger over the lasting implications of Cuomo's resignation on New York's political landscape and the broader movement against sexual harassment.
