Britain Warns Taliban Against Using Afghanistan as Terrorist Base

LONDON, Aug 17 â€“ Britain has issued a stark warning to the Taliban, emphasizing that Afghanistan must not become a launching pad for terror attacks against the West. This comes amid growing concerns over the potential for militants from al Qaeda and the Islamic State to establish a foothold in the country following the Taliban's swift return to power.

In light of the evolving situation, UK Foreign Secretary Dominic Raab voiced the necessity of adopting a pragmatic stance towards the new de facto rulers of Afghanistan. Speaking to reporters, Raab highlighted the importance of attempting to influence the Taliban towards inclusivity. "We need to try and shape what comes next through pragmatic engagement, holding the Taliban to their commitment that they want an inclusive government," he said.

The Foreign Secretary stressed the utility of both diplomatic and economic measures as tools to exert leverage over the Taliban. Despite describing them as a "red tag bunch of thugs," Raab acknowledged the reality of having to deal with them as the current power in Afghanistan. "We have to be realistic about engaging with the new realities on the ground," he added.

Raab's strategy involves testing and guiding the Taliban regime towards moderation, though he admits this is no small task. Stability at Kabul airport, essential for evacuations and aid, is beginning to materialize according to Raab. "Stability there is absolutely crucial, and we're starting to see some signs of that," he mentioned, hopeful for a smoother operation moving forward.

The Foreign Secretary clarified that, as of now, direct talks with the Taliban are not on the table, but he didn't dismiss the possibility of dialogue in the future. "At the moment there's no immediate prospect of sitting down face to face, but that doesn't mean we won't engage in some form through third parties," Raab stated, indicating a cautious but open stance towards interaction.

In the midst of these diplomatic efforts, Prince Harry has made an appeal to veterans, encouraging them to rally around each other in light of the developments in Afghanistan. Having served two tours in Afghanistan himself and founding the Invictus Games, Prince Harry is acutely aware of how the current conflict impacts the military community, especially those who participated in the conflict directly.

"To the military community, I want to remind you, 'please check in on each other and offer support,'" Prince Harry urged. Highlighting the significant impact of the situation on the Invictus community, he added, "We made a commitment to those who served. We must make sure that commitment is honored."

As Britain navigates these challenging waters with the Taliban, the focus remains on securing the safety and rights of the Afghan population while preventing the country from reverting to a haven for international terror.
