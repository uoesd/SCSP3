The couple, married in 2016, the same year Ryan joined the Army, had a relationship grounded in mutual love and support. Despite the distance and danger inherent in his profession, their bond remained unshakeable. "My last message to him was telling him how much I love him and asking him to reply when he could," said Alena, a poignant reminder of their final, interrupted conversation.

Ryan C. Knauss's dedication to his role was undeniable. Alena fondly remembers him as a charismatic individual, always eager to lend a hand and uplift those around him. "He had a way of lighting up a room and making everyone feel valued," she stated, emphasizing the profound impact he had on the lives of others.

Despite the overwhelming grief, Alena finds comfort in her memories and the legacy her husband leaves behind. "Ryan was incredibly dedicated to his field. If he knew the outcome, he would do it all over again," she affirmed, highlighting his unwavering commitment to serving his country and protecting others.

As the nation mourns the loss of the 13 service members, Alena's story provides a personal glimpse into the sacrifices made by those involved in the Kabul airport operation, and the enduring strength of the families they left behind.
