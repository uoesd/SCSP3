Harry and Meghan's Financial Independence: From Royal Funding to Hollywood Millions

In an unprecedented move that captured the world's attention, Prince Harry and Meghan Markle, also known as the Duke and Duchess of Sussex, stepped down as senior members of the British royal family in early 2020. The couple announced their intention to become financially independent and relocated to California, marking a new chapter in their lives.

Before their departure from royal duties, Harry and Meghan's lifestyle was largely funded by the Duchy of Cornwall, a vast estate that provided about 95% of their income, with the remaining 5% coming from the Sovereign Grant. This funding supported their work on behalf of the Queen and covered their official residence's expenses.

Seeking a fresh start in the United States, Harry and Meghan purchased a lavish $14.7 million estate in Montecito, California. This new chapter came with significant financial responsibilities, including an estimated annual security cost of around $3 million, a necessity due to their high profile.

Initially, Prince Harry's financial independence was buoyed by a $13 million trust from his late mother, Princess Diana. Meghan contributed $2 million from her successful acting career, notably from her role in the television series "Suits". Despite their considerable resources, the couple's ambition to become self-sufficient in their finances has led them to explore various avenues.

Their net worth has seen significant growth thanks to lucrative contracts within the entertainment industry. In September 2020, Harry and Meghan signed a groundbreaking $100 million contract with Netflix to produce a range of productions, from documentaries to children's programming.
