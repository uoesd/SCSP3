Amid rising tensions and growing calls for accountability, Republican Senator Lisa Murkowski has emerged as one of several Republicans criticizing the president's actions. Murkowski has gone a step further, calling for Trump's resignation, highlighting the severity of the situation and the bipartisan concern over the president's conduct.

Despite the growing unrest, President Trump has promised a smooth transfer of power to President-elect Joe Biden, although he has made it clear that he will not attend Biden's inauguration. This decision marks a rare presidential snub, underscoring the deep divisions and turmoil that have marked the final days of his presidency.

In an extraordinary move, Speaker Pelosi revealed that she had discussed steps to prevent Trump from initiating military hostilities or accessing the nuclear launch codes with Gen. Mark Milley, the Chairman of the Joint Chiefs of Staff. This discussion underscores the grave concerns among some of the nation's highest-ranking officials regarding the president's state of mind and his capacity to harm national security.

The draft impeachment articles accuse Trump of abuse of power in relation to his role in inciting the Capitol siege, presenting a clear and direct charge against the president. These unfolding events set the stage for a House vote on impeachment that could take place as early as Wednesday, signaling an unprecedented effort to hold the president accountable.

Responding to the impeachment efforts, White House spokesman Judd Deere criticized the move as divisive. "A politically motivated impeachment against a President with 12 days remaining in his term will only serve to further divide our great country," Deere stated, echoing the administration's position on the issue.

Amid this political turmoil, Twitter has taken the significant step of permanently suspending President Trump's account, citing a "risk of further incitement of violence." This decision represents a major blow to Trump's ability to communicate directly with his followers and has sparked debates over the role of social media platforms in public discourse.

House Speaker Pelosi and Senate Minority Leader Chuck Schumer have also considered invoking the 25th Amendment to remove Trump from office. However, with the recent string of resignations in the Cabinet, this path seems increasingly unlikely, leaving impeachment as the most viable option to address the crisis.

Democratic Representative Adam Schiff of California, a key figure in the president's first impeachment, expressed his support for the action, stating, "Every day that he remains in office, he is a danger to the Republic."

Independent Senator Bernie Sanders of Vermont also weighed in on the matter, emphasizing the need for accountability. "It must be made clear that no president, now or in the future, can lead an insurrection against the U.S. government," Sanders urged.

As Congress moves forward with impeachment proceedings, the nation watches closely, waiting to see how this momentous effort to address the Capitol siege and hold President Trump accountable will unfold in the days leading up to the inauguration of Joe Biden as the 46th President of the United States.
