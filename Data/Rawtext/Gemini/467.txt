Paparazzi Pursue Harry and Meghan in California, Sparking Legal Battles

Since their relocation to California, Prince Harry and Meghan Markle have faced relentless paparazzi attention, leading to multiple legal actions and heightened concerns for their privacy.

Paparazzi Incidents and Legal Actions

The couple has experienced at least eight paparazzi incidents since moving to the United States, according to their lawyer, Michael J. Kump. These incidents have included unauthorized photographs taken during private moments, such as a trip to a dentist and a visit with friends.

In response to these intrusions, Harry and Meghan have filed two lawsuits against picture agencies. One lawsuit targeted Splash News and Picture Agency for publishing unsolicited photographs of the couple. The other lawsuit was filed against X17 for using a drone to photograph their son, Archie, without their consent.

"This action is necessary to protect our son's privacy and to uncover those who are profiting from these illegal actions," Kump said in a statement.

Unauthorized Photographs and Public Outings

Despite their efforts to maintain privacy, photographs of Harry and Meghan have been widely distributed. They have been photographed in Beverly Hills, delivering parcels for Project Angel Food, and on a recent outing with friends David Foster and Katharine McPhee.

Carpool Karaoke Appearance

One notable incident involved Harry's appearance on James Corden's "The Late Late Show" for a COVID-safe edition of Carpool Karaoke. The episode featured the couple singing along to popular songs while adhering to social distancing guidelines.

Beverly Hills Mansion and Tyler Perry's Loan

Early in their California stay, Harry and Meghan were photographed near the Beverly Hills mansion they were staying in, which was loaned to them by Tyler Perry. The couple has since moved to a more secluded location.

Drone Photography and X17 Settlement

The lawsuit against X17 for using a drone to photograph Archie resulted in an apology and settlement from the picture agency. The settlement included a commitment to refrain from using drones to photograph the couple's children.

Protecting Privacy and Legal Recourse

Harry and Meghan's legal actions have sent a clear message that they will not tolerate unauthorized photography of their family. Their lawsuits have aimed to protect their privacy and hold accountable those who engage in illegal activities.
