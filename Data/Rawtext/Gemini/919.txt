'Man in the Arena: Tom Brady' Docuseries to Premiere on ESPN+ November 16

ESPN+ will debut the highly anticipated docuseries 'Man in the Arena: Tom Brady' on November 16, offering an unprecedented look into the legendary quarterback's remarkable career.

Directed by Gotham Chopra of Religion of Sports, the 10-part series chronicles Brady's 10 Super Bowl appearances and other career milestones through the lens of his emotional and psychological journey.

"This series is an inside look into my 22-year journey in the NFL," said Brady. "It's a very personal look at the highs and lows of my career, and I'm excited for people to see it."

Each episode focuses on a different Super Bowl appearance, beginning with Super Bowl XXXVI, where Brady led the New England Patriots to their first championship. The series delves into the challenges, triumphs, and sacrifices that shaped Brady's path to becoming one of the greatest quarterbacks of all time.

"Tom Brady is one of the most iconic athletes in sports history," said Chopra. "This series is a chance to go behind the scenes and explore the human side of his epic journey."

Co-produced by ESPN, Religion of Sports, 199 Productions, and NFL Films, the series features exclusive interviews with key figures from Brady's life and career, including his family, coaches, and teammates.

"This series highlights Tom Brady's epic journey in sports and his complex character," said Brian Lockhart, Senior Vice President, ESPN+ Original Content and ESPN Films. "It's a must-watch for any sports fan."

In addition to the docuseries, ESPN+ will offer companion programming, including the 'Inside the Arena' post-show, 'After the Arena', and the 'Man in the Arena' podcast series.

New episodes of 'Man in the Arena: Tom Brady' will be released weekly on Tuesdays, with a special presentation at 9 p.m. ET followed by 'Inside the Arena'.
