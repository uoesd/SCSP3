Biden and Harris Inaugurated, Call for Unity and Hope

Washington, D.C. - President Joe Biden and Vice President Kamala Harris took the oath of office on Wednesday, January 20, 2021, marking a historic day for the United States. The inauguration, held under unprecedented security measures, was celebrated with a prime-time televised event hosted by actor Tom Hanks.

Inaugural Addresses

In his inaugural address, President Biden emphasized the importance of democracy and called for unity, repeating the message he had delivered in his earlier speech.

"This is America's day. This is democracy's day. A day of history and hope, of renewal and resolve," Biden said. "Through a crucible for the ages, America has been tested anew and America has risen to the challenge."

Vice President Harris, in her speech, spoke about the power of "American aspiration" and her historical significance as the first female, Black, and Asian vice president.

"We are the heirs of a long and heroic struggle to give life to the words of our founding documents," Harris said. "We are a nation that can be defined in a single word: possibilities."

First Actions as President

Biden wasted no time in tackling his first actions as president. He signed executive orders on immigration, the coronavirus pandemic, and rejoining the Paris climate accord.

Break from Tradition

Former President Donald Trump broke with tradition by not attending the inauguration. He did, however, write Biden a "very generous letter," according to Biden.

New White House Press Secretary

New White House press secretary Jen Psaki promised truth and transparency, announcing daily press briefings.

"We are committed to providing the American people with the information they need to understand the work of their government," Psaki said.

Heightened Security

Security was heightened around the Capitol, with over 25,000 National Guard members deployed. However, no major security incidents were reported.

Traditional Events

The day included traditional events such as the "Pass in Review" inspection of troops. The ceremony was marked by calls for unity against an "uncivil war" of political divisions.

Conclusion

The inauguration of President Joe Biden and Vice President Kamala Harris marked a historic day for the United States. The day was filled with hope, unity, and a call to action to address the challenges facing the nation.
