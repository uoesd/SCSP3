Vice President Harris Delivers Powerful Address at Hampton University, Underscoring the Critical Role of HBCUs in Shaping the Future

Hampton, VA - In a resounding speech that resonated with the legacy and aspirations of Historically Black Colleges and Universities (HBCUs), Vice President Kamala Harris took to the stage at Hampton University, emphasizing the pivotal role these institutions play in shaping the nation's future, particularly in the fields of science, technology, engineering, and mathematics (STEM).

"HBCUs have been beacons of opportunity and excellence for generations," declared Vice President Harris, her voice echoing through the historic campus. "They have played an indispensable role in educating our nation's leaders, innovators, and change-makers, and their contributions to our national security, scientific research, and development are immeasurable."

The Vice President highlighted the enduring partnership between Hampton University and NASA, a collaboration that has resulted in the launch of satellites that collect valuable atmospheric data. She commended the university's commitment to STEM education and its unwavering dedication to preparing students for careers in these critical fields.

"The challenges we face today, from pandemics to climate change, are interconnected and interdependent," Harris noted. "They demand a diverse and inclusive workforce, one that reflects the richness of our nation and brings a multitude of perspectives to the table."

She emphasized the historical significance of HBCUs in educating leaders and innovators, citing the countless graduates who have gone on to make profound contributions to society. "From the halls of Hampton University have emerged scientists, engineers, doctors, lawyers, and leaders who have shaped the course of our nation," she said.

Vice President Harris stressed the importance of investing in HBCUs, recognizing that these institutions are essential for the nation's strength and prosperity. She pointed to the historic investments in research and development through the Build Back agenda, highlighting the Biden-Harris administration's commitment to supporting HBCUs and their vital mission.

"Diversity in STEM fields is not just a matter of fairness; it is a matter of national security and economic competitiveness," Harris asserted. "We need a workforce that reflects the diversity of our nation, particularly women and people of color, to ensure that we are harnessing the full potential of our human capital."

She discussed the potential of artificial intelligence (AI) and the necessity for a diverse group of people teaching the machines to ensure equitable outcomes. "AI is a powerful tool that can be used for good or for ill," she said. "It is imperative that we have a diverse group of people involved in developing and deploying AI systems to ensure that they are fair, unbiased, and used for the benefit of all."

Vice President Harris concluded her address by reaffirming the critical role of HBCUs in addressing national and global challenges. "HBCUs are not just educational institutions; they are pillars of our communities," she said. "They are places where students learn not only academic skills but also the values of leadership, service, and social justice."

"Support for HBCUs is essential for societal participation and self-determination," Harris emphasized. "By investing in these institutions, we are investing in the future of our nation and the well-being of our communities."

The Vice President's speech was met with thunderous applause and a standing ovation from the audience, a testament to the profound impact that HBCUs have had on the nation and the world. Her words resonated with the students, faculty, and community members in attendance, inspiring them to continue the legacy of excellence and innovation that has defined HBCUs for generations.
