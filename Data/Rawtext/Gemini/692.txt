In a motion filed in court, prosecutors argue that the Crumbleys are flight risks, citing their financial troubles and previous attempt to flee. They also allege that the parents knew about Ethan's depression and fascination with guns but neglected his well-being.

"The defendants were aware of the defendant's disturbing behavior and mental health issues, including his fascination with guns," the motion states. "Despite this knowledge, the defendants failed to take any meaningful steps to address their son's mental health issues or to prevent him from accessing firearms."

Prosecutors allege that the Crumbleys were too consumed with their own lives to attend to Ethan, who exhibited disturbing behavior.

"The defendants were more concerned with their own personal lives than with the well-being of their son," the motion states. "They failed to provide him with the necessary supervision, guidance, and support."

Allegedly, Ethan's troubles were known to his parents; he was sadder than usual, tortured animals, and they bought him a gun despite this.

"The defendants knew or should have known that their son was a danger to himself and others," the motion states. "They failed to take any meaningful steps to prevent him from harming himself or others."

The filing criticizes the Crumbleys for not being more vigilant on the day of the shooting, especially since they had bought Ethan a gun.

"On the day of the shooting, the defendants were aware that their son had access to a firearm," the motion states. "They failed to take any steps to prevent him from using that firearm to harm others."

Oakland County, Michigan, Chief Assistant Prosecutor David Williams said in a statement that the Crumbleys "failed to take reasonable steps to prevent their son from committing this horrific act of violence."

"They knew or should have known that their son was a danger to himself and others," Williams said. "They failed to take any meaningful steps to prevent him from harming himself or others."

The Crumbleys' attorneys have denied the allegations, saying that they are "loving parents" who did everything they could to help their son.

"The Crumbleys are innocent of these charges," said Shannon Smith, an attorney for James Crumbley. "They are loving parents who did everything they could to help their son."
