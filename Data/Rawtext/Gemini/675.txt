Rittenhouse is charged with the fatal shooting of two men and wounding another during police brutality protests in Kenosha last year. Prosecutors argue that the evidence they are seeking to introduce is relevant to Rittenhouse's state of mind and his propensity for violence.

In a motion filed on Friday, prosecutors asked the court to admit a video from July 1, 2020, showing Rittenhouse striking a teenage girl in the head with a plastic bag during a protest in Kenosha. The motion also includes photos from January 2021 showing Rittenhouse with members of the Proud Boys, making a white power symbol gesture.

Prosecutors argue that the video and photos demonstrate Rittenhouse's pattern of seeking out violent situations and his willingness to commit further acts of violence. They also allege that Rittenhouse's association with the Proud Boys, a group known for its violent rhetoric and actions, further supports their claim that he is a danger to society.

Rittenhouse's attorneys have opposed the motion, arguing that the evidence is irrelevant and prejudicial. They claim that the video of the incident with the teenage girl was taken out of context and that Rittenhouse was acting in self-defense. They also argue that Rittenhouse's association with the Proud Boys is not relevant to the charges against him.

The judge has not yet ruled on the motion.

Rittenhouse's trial is scheduled to start on Nov. 1. He has pleaded not guilty to all charges.

The case has drawn national attention and has become a flashpoint in the debate over gun rights and racial justice. Black Lives Matter supporters view Rittenhouse as a white supremacist who traveled to Kenosha to incite violence, while some conservatives see him as a symbol for gun rights and self-defense.

The outcome of the trial is likely to have a significant impact on the ongoing debate over these issues.
