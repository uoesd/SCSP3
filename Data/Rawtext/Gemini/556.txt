Ferishta's Journey of Hope and Resilience

Ferishta's journey to reunite with her family has been marked by both unimaginable pain and unwavering determination. After learning of the bombing, she frantically searched for her loved ones, fearing the worst.

"I couldn't believe it," she said. "I thought I had lost them forever."

When she finally received word that Ahmad Faisal and Mina had survived, a glimmer of hope emerged amidst the darkness. However, the realization that her sister had been killed shattered her heart.

"It's still so hard to believe that she's gone," Ferishta said. "She was such a wonderful mother."

Despite her grief, Ferishta remains focused on providing a safe and loving home for her niece and nephew. She is grateful for the support of her community and the medical professionals who are caring for Ahmad Faisal and Mina.

"I know we have a long road ahead of us," she said. "But I'm determined to give these children the best life possible."

Bradley's Commitment to Helping Afghan Families

Retired Air Force Lt. Gen. John Bradley has dedicated his post-military career to assisting Afghan families in need. Through the Lamia Afghan Foundation, he has provided support to refugees, orphans, and victims of war.

As Ahmad Faisal and Mina begin their recovery, they are surrounded by love and support. Their aunt, Ferishta, is determined to provide them with a stable and nurturing environment where they can heal and thrive.
