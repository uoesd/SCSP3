Tragedy at Kabul Airport: 13 American Troops Killed in ISIS-K Suicide Bombing

In a devastating turn of events, a suicide bombing claimed the lives of nearly 200 people at the Hamid Karzai International Airport in Kabul, Afghanistan, on August 26, 2021. Among the victims were 13 American service members, who were part of the ongoing evacuation efforts.

The attack, carried out by an ISIS-K suicide bomber, also resulted in the deaths of at least 170 Afghans, two British nationals, and the child of a British citizen. The incident has sent shockwaves through the international community and has been condemned by leaders around the world.

President Biden's Statement

President Joe Biden expressed his condolences to the families of the fallen service members, describing them as "heroes" who were engaged in a dangerous mission. "These American service members who gave their lives were heroes," Biden said. "They were engaged in a dangerous, selfless mission to save the lives of others."

The Fallen American Service Members

The American victims included 11 Marines, an Army soldier, and a Navy medic. Their names and ranks are as follows:

  • Marine Corps Lance Cpl. David Espinoza, 20, of Rio Bravo, Texas
  • Marine Corps Lance Cpl. Rylee McCollum, 20, of Jackson, Wyoming
  • Marine Corps Lance Cpl. Kareem Nikoui, 20, of Norco, California
  • Marine Corps Cpl. Hunter Lopez, 22, of Indio, California
  • Marine Corps Sgt. Nicole Gee, 23, of Roseville, California
  • Marine Corps Staff Sgt. Darin Hoover, 31, of Salt Lake City, Utah
  • Marine Corps Staff Sgt. Ryan Knauss, 23, of Knoxville, Tennessee
  • Marine Corps Cpl. Humberto Sanchez, 22, of Logansport, Indiana
  • Marine Corps Lance Cpl. Jared Schmitz, 20, of St. Charles, Missouri
  • Marine Corps Lance Cpl. Dylan Merola, 20, of Rancho Cucamonga, California
  • Marine Corps Lance Cpl. Daegan Page, 20, of Omaha, Nebraska
  • Navy Hospitalman Maxton Soviak, 22, of Berlin Heights, Ohio
  • Army Staff Sgt. Ryan Knauss, 23, of Knoxville, Tennessee

Tributes to the Fallen

Adm. Mike Gilday, Chief of Naval Operations, hailed the service members' courage and sacrifice. "Their bravery and selflessness represent the very best of the United States Navy and Marine Corps," Gilday said. "They will never be forgotten."

Rachel Soviak, mother of Navy Corpsman Maxton Soviak, spoke of her son's pride in serving his country. "He was so proud to be a Navy corpsman," Soviak said. "He loved his country and he loved his job."

Rep. Henry Cuellar (D-Texas) expressed his condolences to the families of the fallen service members. "These brave men and women were serving our country with honor and distinction," Cuellar said. "Their sacrifice will never be forgotten."
