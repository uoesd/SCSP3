Vanderbilt Kicker Sarah Fuller to Participate in Biden's Inauguration Event

Nashville, TN - Vanderbilt University kicker Sarah Fuller will join a star-studded lineup at President-elect Joe Biden's inauguration event, titled "Celebrating America." The primetime special, hosted by Tom Hanks, will feature performances by Foo Fighters, John Legend, and Bruce Springsteen, among others.

The special, scheduled to air Wednesday night following Biden's swearing-in, aims to celebrate a new national journey toward a united America. Eva Longoria and Kerry Washington will introduce segments highlighting young community leaders.

Fuller, who made history in December as the first woman to score in a Power 5 football game, expressed her excitement and honor in being part of the inauguration on Twitter. She highlighted the event's significance for American women and girls, urging to #LeadLikeAWoman.

"I'm so excited and honored to be a part of the 'Celebrating America' event for President-elect Biden's inauguration," Fuller tweeted. "This is a historic moment for our country, and I'm proud to represent women and girls everywhere."

The inauguration special will be broadcasted across major networks, including ABC, CBS, CNN, MSNBC, and NBC, and streamed on various platforms, including YouTube, Facebook, and Twitter.

Fuller's participation in the event marks a significant milestone for women in sports and beyond. Her historic achievement in December inspired countless young girls and women, demonstrating that anything is possible with determination and perseverance.

The "Celebrating America" special will showcase a diverse range of voices and perspectives, reflecting the unity and hope that Biden's presidency represents. The event will feature performances by artists from various genres, including country, rock, and pop, as well as speeches from prominent figures.

The inauguration of Joe Biden and Kamala Harris marks a new chapter in American history.
