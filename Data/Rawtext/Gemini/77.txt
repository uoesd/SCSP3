Capitol Police Officer Brian Sicknick Honored in Solemn Capitol Rotunda Ceremony

Washington, D.C. - Capitol Police Officer Brian Sicknick, who died from injuries sustained during the January 6 Capitol riot, was memorialized in a solemn ceremony at the U.S. Capitol on Wednesday.

Congressional leaders, including House Speaker Nancy Pelosi and Senate Majority Leader Chuck Schumer, paid tribute to Sicknick in the Capitol Rotunda. Pelosi praised Sicknick as "a patriot whose sacrifice will always be remembered."

"Officer Sicknick was a hero who gave his life to protect our democracy," Pelosi said. "His sacrifice will never be forgotten."

Schumer described Sicknick as "a good, kind man" and highlighted the personal loss felt by many. "Brian was a friend to everyone he met," Schumer said. "He will be deeply missed."

Sicknick's family attended the ceremony, alongside members of Congress. President Joe Biden and First Lady Jill Biden paid their respects to Sicknick on Tuesday night.

A viewing session was held for Capitol Police officers and others to honor Sicknick. His remains were to be buried at Arlington National Cemetery following the memorial service.

Pelosi and Schumer honored Sicknick's heroism on January 6, stating he helped protect democracy. "Officer Sicknick stood his ground against the mob that attacked our Capitol," Pelosi said. "He put his life on the line to defend our democracy."

Schumer added, "Brian Sicknick was a true American hero. He will forever be remembered for his bravery and sacrifice."

Brian Sicknick is only the fifth person to lie in honor in the Capitol Rotunda, a recognition of his service and sacrifice. The other four individuals were President Abraham Lincoln, President John F. Kennedy, President Ronald Reagan, and Capitol Police Officer Jacob Chestnut.

The ceremony was a somber reminder of the violence that occurred on January 6 and the sacrifices made by those who defended the Capitol. Officer Sicknick's memory will serve as a reminder of the importance of protecting our democracy and the brave men and women who serve to keep us safe.
