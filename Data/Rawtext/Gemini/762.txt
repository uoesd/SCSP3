Dolph Lundgren Expresses Shock, Advocates Gun Safety After 'Rust' Movie Set Tragedy

Los Angeles, CA - Renowned actor Dolph Lundgren has expressed his shock and deep concern over the tragic shooting incident that occurred on the set of the Western film 'Rust' involving Alec Baldwin. The incident resulted in the death of cinematographer Halyna Hutchins and injured director Joel Souza.

Lundgren's Shock and Concern

"I was absolutely shocked and devastated when I heard about the tragedy on the 'Rust' set," Lundgren said in an exclusive interview. "My heart goes out to Halyna Hutchins' family and everyone affected by this terrible accident."

Emphasis on Firearm Safety

Lundgren, who has starred in numerous action films, emphasized the paramount importance of firearm safety on movie sets. "We have very strict protocols in place for handling firearms on set," he explained. "We always triple-check the guns, make sure they're unloaded, and follow all the necessary safety procedures."

Recalling the 'The Crow' Tragedy

Lundgren recalled a similar tragedy that occurred during the filming of 'The Crow' in 1993, where actor Brandon Lee was accidentally killed by a live round. "That incident was a wake-up call for the industry," he said. "It showed us that we can never be too careful when it comes to firearm safety."

Advocating for Gun Safety

Lundgren, a strong advocate for gun safety, believes that stricter measures need to be implemented to prevent such tragedies from happening again. "We need to have more stringent safety protocols, better training, and more oversight on movie sets," he said. "We can't afford to have any more accidents like this."

CGI for Gun Scenes

In his upcoming directorial project 'Castle Falls,' Lundgren has opted to use CGI for all gun scenes. "I don't want to take any unnecessary risks," he explained. "CGI allows us to create realistic gun effects without putting anyone in danger."

Baldwin's Accident and Investigation

On October 21, 2021, Alec Baldwin was handling a Colt revolver provided by assistant director Dave Halls when it discharged a live round, striking Hutchins and Souza. Baldwin has since vowed to cooperate fully with the police investigation and expressed his condolences to Hutchins' family.

Criminal Investigation and Legal Actions

The Santa Fe County Sheriff's Department is conducting a criminal investigation into the incident. Sheriff Adan Mendoza stated that "all options are on the table" regarding potential charges.

Legal actions have also been taken against Baldwin and other crew members by affected parties. Script supervisor Mamie Mitchell filed a lawsuit alleging negligence and unsafe working conditions. Set's gaffer Serge Svetnoy filed a similar lawsuit, claiming that he suffered emotional distress and physical injuries as a result of the incident.

Baldwin's Statement

In a statement released after the incident, Baldwin expressed his profound sorrow and shock. "There are no words to convey my shock and sadness regarding the tragic accident that took the life of Halyna Hutchins," he said. "I'm fully cooperating with the police investigation and will continue to do so."

Industry Response

The 'Rust' shooting has sent shockwaves through the entertainment industry, prompting calls for stricter safety measures and a re-evaluation of firearm use on movie sets. Many actors, directors, and producers have expressed their condolences and support for Hutchins' family and the injured crew members.

Conclusion

The tragic 'Rust' movie set shooting has highlighted the critical importance of firearm safety on movie sets. Dolph Lundgren's advocacy for stricter measures and his decision to use CGI for gun scenes in his upcoming project are examples of the industry's commitment to preventing such tragedies from happening again. The ongoing investigation and legal actions will determine the full extent of responsibility and accountability for this devastating incident.
