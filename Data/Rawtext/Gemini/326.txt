Fact Check: Did the Ever Given Cargo Ship Draw a Crude Image in the Suez Canal?

An image circulating on social media purportedly shows the Ever Given cargo ship making a route that resembles a crude drawing of a penis. While the image is genuine, there is no evidence to suggest the ship's route was intentionally designed to resemble such a drawing.

The Ever Given blocked the Suez Canal on March 23, 2021, disrupting global shipping traffic. Following the incident, the image of the ship's route was widely shared on social media.

VesselFinder.com, a vessel-trafficking service provider, created a video simulation showing the ship's movements before the incident. A spokesperson for VesselFinder confirmed to Vice that the route was genuine and dismissed conspiracy theories.

"There's no room for some kind of conspiracies or false data," the spokesperson said.

Myshiptracking.com also shows the Ever Given's route resembling a NSFW drawing.

CNN reported that eight tugboats were trying to free the Ever Given, indicating significant disruption to the Suez Canal traffic.

The image of the Ever Given's route has sparked speculation and amusement online. However, it is important to note that there is no evidence to suggest the ship's route was intentionally designed to resemble a crude drawing.

The Ever Given's route is a genuine representation of the ship's movements before it got stuck in the Suez Canal. The image has been widely shared on social media, but there is no evidence to suggest it was intentionally designed to resemble a crude drawing.
