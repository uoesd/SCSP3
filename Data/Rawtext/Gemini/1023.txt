Winter Storm Lana Unleashes Fury on Texas, Louisiana, and Beyond

Winter Storm Lana has left a trail of disruption across parts of Texas, Louisiana, Mississippi, Alabama, and Arkansas, bringing heavy snowfall, power outages, and hazardous road conditions.

Texas Bears the Brunt of the Storm

Texas has been particularly hard-hit by the storm, with widespread snowfall and power outages. Some areas have received more than half a foot of snow, causing significant travel disruptions. Over 150,000 homes and businesses have been left without electricity, with over 100,000 of these outages in Texas alone.

In Tyler, Texas, heavy snowfall caused a tree to fall, damaging a home and two cars. Fortunately, no injuries were reported. Schools across the state have either canceled classes or delayed openings due to the storm.

Louisiana Grapples with Snow and Ice

Louisiana has also been impacted by the storm, with heavy snowfall and icy road conditions. Louisiana State Police reported numerous crashes and assisted with disabled vehicles due to slick roads. Interstates 49 and 220 near Shreveport, Louisiana, were closed due to the weather, along with several state highways.

Louisiana closed state government offices in 29 parishes in response to the storm, while Hinds County, Mississippi, delayed opening government offices. Road crews in Jackson, Mississippi, treated icy bridges, and warnings of icy conditions extended to the Tennessee and Alabama state lines.

Alabama and Arkansas See Snowfall and Road Closures

The storm also impacted roads in north and north-central Alabama, with continued snowfall reported in several counties. Schools in Alabama and Arkansas have also canceled or delayed classes due to the storm.
