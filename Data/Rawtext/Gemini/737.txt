Zero Tolerance Policy

The zero-tolerance policy prohibits any form of threats, including verbal, written, or electronic, and applies to all students, staff, and visitors. Any individual found in violation will face immediate suspension or expulsion.

"We want to make it clear that threats of any kind will not be tolerated," said Lemond. "We must send a strong message that such behavior is unacceptable and will have serious consequences."

Increased Law Enforcement Presence

Law enforcement officers will be present in all Oxford schools during school hours, providing a visible deterrent and immediate response to any incidents. The district has also hired a security firm to provide additional guards at all schools.

"We are grateful for the support of our local law enforcement agencies," said Lemond. "Their presence will help to reassure our students and staff that they are safe."

Backpack Ban

Backpacks will be prohibited in all Oxford schools. Students will be allowed to carry clear backpacks or small bags that can be easily searched.

"Backpacks can be used to conceal weapons or other dangerous items," said Lemond. "By prohibiting them, we are reducing the potential for threats to enter our schools."

Criticism and Concerns

Community Trauma and Fear

The shooting has left a deep scar on the Oxford community. Students and staff are experiencing trauma and fear for their safety. The district is providing counseling services and other support to help them cope with the aftermath.

"Our community is hurting," said Lemond. "We are doing everything we can to support our students, staff, and families during this difficult time."

Court Proceedings Delayed

The court proceedings for Ethan Crumbley and his parents have been delayed to give the Michigan community time to heal and prepare. The next hearing is scheduled for January 7th, 2022.

School Closures
