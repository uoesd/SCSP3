Alec Baldwin to Make First Public Appearance Since 'Rust' Shooting at Ripple of Hope Award Event

New York, NY - Actor Alec Baldwin is set to make his first public appearance since the fatal shooting on the set of the film "Rust" at the 2021 Ripple of Hope Award event. The event, organized by the Robert F. Kennedy Human Rights organization, will honor leaders who have demonstrated a commitment to social change and reflect Robert Kennedy's values of equality, justice, and human rights.

Baldwin will join a distinguished group of honorees at the event, including Stacey Abrams, José E. Feliciano, Amanda Gorman, Deven Parekh, and Hans Vestberg. Vice President Kamala Harris will deliver the keynote address.

The Ripple of Hope Award event will feature a mix of in-person and virtual experiences and will be held at the New York Hilton Midtown Manhattan Hotel. Past laureates of the award include Dr. Anthony Fauci and Colin Kaepernick.

Baldwin's appearance at the event comes after he gave a tell-all interview about the fatal shooting incident, in which he stated that he did not pull the trigger. The incident is currently under investigation to determine how a live round was present in the gun.

According to Baldwin, the gun discharged without him pulling the trigger while he was being directed on its positioning.

The Ripple of Hope Award

The Ripple of Hope Award was established in 1984 to honor individuals who have dedicated their lives to advancing social justice and human rights. The award is named after Robert F. Kennedy, who was a passionate advocate for equality and justice.

Past recipients of the Ripple of Hope Award include:

  • Desmond Tutu
  • Nelson Mandela
  • Muhammad Ali
  • Bill Clinton
  • Hillary Clinton
  • Barack Obama
  • Malala Yousafzai
  • Greta Thunberg

Alec Baldwin's Involvement in the 'Rust' Shooting

On October 21, 2021, cinematographer Halyna Hutchins was fatally shot on the set of the film "Rust" after a prop gun held by Baldwin discharged. The incident has sparked a wide-ranging investigation into the safety protocols on the set and the presence of live ammunition.

Baldwin has denied any wrongdoing and has stated that he did not know the gun contained live ammunition. He has also expressed his condolences to Hutchins' family and has said that he is cooperating with the investigation.

The Investigation into the 'Rust' Shooting

The investigation into the "Rust" shooting is ongoing, and no charges have been filed. The Santa Fe County Sheriff's Office is leading the investigation, with the assistance of the FBI.

The investigation is focused on determining how a live round of ammunition ended up in the prop gun and who is responsible for the safety protocols on the set.

Baldwin's appearance at the Ripple of Hope Award event will be his first public appearance since the "Rust" shooting. It is unclear what he will say or do at the event, but it is likely that he will address the shooting and its aftermath.
