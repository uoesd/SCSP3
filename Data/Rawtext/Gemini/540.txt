Thirteen U.S. Service Members Killed in Kabul Airport Attack Identified

CNN – The Department of Defense has released the names of the 13 U.S. service members killed in the horrific attack at Kabul's airport on Thursday. The fallen heroes include:

  • Army Staff Sgt. Ryan Knauss
  • US Marine Rylee McCollum
  • Cpl. Daegan Page
  • US Navy corpsman Maxton Soviak
  • Marine Corps Cpl. Hunter Lopez
  • Marine Corps Lance Cpl. Jared M. Schmitz
  • Marine Corps Sgt. Nicole L. Gee

Army Staff Sgt. Ryan Knauss

Army Staff Sgt. Ryan Knauss was honored by US Rep. Tim Burchett (R-TN) for his heroism in the face of danger. Knauss, 23, was the only Army member killed in the attack.

"Ryan Knauss was a hero who gave his life to save others," Burchett said in a statement. "He will be remembered for his bravery and sacrifice."

Knauss's family described him as a "loving son, brother, and friend" who was "always willing to help others."

US Marine Rylee McCollum

US Marine Rylee McCollum, 20, was on his first deployment when he was killed in the attack. He was expecting to become a father in just three weeks.

McCollum's family said he was "a loving son, brother, and friend" who was "always smiling and making others laugh."

"He was so excited to be a father," his sister, Roice McCollum, told CNN. "He was going to be the best dad."

Cpl. Daegan Page

Cpl. Daegan Page, 23, was remembered by his family for his compassionate nature and plans for the future beyond his military service.

"Daegan was a kind and loving soul who always put others before himself," his family said in a statement. "He had a bright future ahead of him, and we are heartbroken that it was cut short."

Page was planning to attend college after his military service and pursue a career in law enforcement.

US Navy corpsman Maxton Soviak

US Navy corpsman Maxton Soviak, 22, was celebrated for his pride in serving as a Navy corpsman attached to the Marines.

"Maxton was a dedicated and compassionate corpsman who loved his job," his family said in a statement. "He was always willing to go the extra mile to help others."

Soviak's family said he was "a true hero" who "will be deeply missed."

Marine Corps Cpl. Hunter Lopez

Marine Corps Cpl. Hunter Lopez, 22, planned to follow in his parents' footsteps and join law enforcement after military service.

"Hunter was a loving son, brother, and friend," his family said in a statement. "He was always smiling and had a positive attitude."

Lopez's family said he was "a true hero" who "will be forever remembered for his sacrifice."

Marine Corps Lance Cpl. Jared M. Schmitz

Marine Corps Lance Cpl. Jared M. Schmitz, 20, was remembered by his father for his dedication to the Marine Corps and special bond with his sister.

"Jared was a great kid," his father, Mark Schmitz, told CNN. "He was always smiling and loved to make people laugh."

Schmitz's father said his son was "a true hero" who "died doing what he loved."

Marine Corps Sgt. Nicole L. Gee

Marine Corps Sgt. Nicole L. Gee, 23, was featured on the Department of Defense's social media for her love of her job, just days before the attack.

"Nicole was a dedicated and compassionate Marine who loved her job," her family said in a statement. "She was always willing to go the extra mile to help others."

Gee's family said she was "a true hero" who "will be deeply missed."

GoFundMe Pages and Public Tributes

GoFundMe pages and public tributes have been set up for the families of the deceased service members. The pages have raised hundreds of thousands of dollars to support the families during this difficult time.

The public is also encouraged to share their condolences and memories of the fallen heroes on social media using the hashtag #KabulHeroes.
