Oxford High School Shooting: Community Mourns the Loss of Four Students

On November 30, 2021, the Oxford High School community was shattered by a tragic shooting that claimed the lives of four beloved students: Tate Myre, Madisyn Baldwin, Hana St. Juliana, and Justin Shilling. The incident also left seven others wounded, including students and a teacher.

Tate Myre: A Shining Star on and off the Field

Tate Myre, a 16-year-old junior, was an honor student and a standout athlete in football and wrestling. His athletic prowess had caught the attention of college recruiters, and he was widely regarded as a pillar of the school community.

Madisyn Baldwin: A Talented Artist and a Loving Sister

Madisyn Baldwin, 17, was a talented artist and a devoted big sister. Her vibrant personality and infectious laugh left an enduring mark on her friends and family.

"Madisyn was a beautiful soul," said her grandmother, Jennifer Graves Mosqueda. "She loved to draw and paint, and she had a heart of gold."

A GoFundMe fundraiser was launched to support Madisyn's family during this difficult time, raising over $100,000 in just a few days.

Hana St. Juliana: A Joyful and Kind Spirit

Hana St. Juliana, 14, was a freshman who brought joy and kindness to everyone she met. She was an active member of the basketball and volleyball teams and was deeply loved by her community.

"Hana was a bright and beautiful young lady," said Pastor Liz Wilson of Immanuel Congregational United Church of Christ. "She had a smile that could melt your heart."

Justin Shilling: A Dedicated Student and Friend

Justin Shilling, 17, was a sophomore who excelled in golf and bowling. He was a dedicated student and a valued friend and co-worker.

"Justin was a great kid," said family friend Jennifer Curtis. "He was always smiling and had a positive attitude."

The Oxford community has rallied together in the wake of this tragedy, offering support to the victims' families and finding ways to honor the memories of those who were lost.

Tributes have poured in from across the state and nation, including messages from President Joe Biden and Michigan Governor Gretchen Whitmer. A memorial service was held at the school's football stadium, where thousands gathered to mourn and pay their respects.
