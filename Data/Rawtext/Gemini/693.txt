Heroic Teenager Tate Myre Sacrificed Himself to Protect Fellow Students in Oxford High School Shooting

Oxford, Michigan - Amidst the unspeakable tragedy that unfolded at Oxford High School on November 30, 2021, the heroic actions of 16-year-old Tate Myre stand as a beacon of courage and selflessness.

Tate, a star athlete and beloved student, confronted the gunman, aiming to protect his fellow students from further harm. His bravery cost him his life, but his legacy will forever inspire those who knew him and countless others.

"Tate was a hero," said JR Laefner, the public address announcer at Oxford football games and a close family friend. "He ran towards the danger to save others."

Tate's selfless nature was evident throughout his life. As a wrestler and football player, he was known for his unwavering determination and willingness to sacrifice for the good of the team.

"Tate was a natural leader," said Ross Wingert, Tate's football and wrestling coach. "He always put the team first, even if it meant sacrificing his own position."

In a testament to his character, Tate shifted positions on the football team from quarterback to wide receiver to fill a need for the team.

"He was a selfless player who always put the team before himself," said Zach Line, Oxford's football coach.

Tate's heroic actions have resonated deeply within the community and beyond. An online petition calling for the renaming of Oxford's Wildcat Stadium in his honor has garnered over 75,000 signatures.

"Tate deserves to be remembered for his bravery and sacrifice," said Kurt Richardson, the coach of Clarkston High School, one of Oxford's football rivals. "Renaming the stadium in his honor would be a fitting tribute to his legacy."

Tony Patritto, the coach of Rochester Adams High School, echoed Richardson's sentiments. "Tate's actions were heroic," he said. "He is an inspiration to us all."

Scott Merchant, the coach of Clinton Township Chippewa Valley High School, added, "Tate's sacrifice will never be forgotten. He is a true hero."

As the community mourns the loss of Tate and the other victims of the shooting, they find solace in his heroic actions. His funeral is scheduled for 10 a.m. Tuesday at Kensington Church in Lake Orion.

"Tate's legacy will live on through the countless lives he touched," said Oakland County Sheriff Mike Bouchard. "He is a hero who will never be forgotten."

Tate Myre's heroic actions serve as a reminder of the indomitable spirit that resides within us all. His sacrifice will forever inspire others to put the well-being of others before their own.
