New Jersey Student Arrested for Threatening SnapChat Message After Michigan School Shooting

In the wake of the tragic school shooting in Oxford, Michigan, a New Jersey high school student has been arrested for posting a threatening message on SnapChat.

Swift Action by Police

Howell Township Police Chief Andrew Kudrick announced the arrest, commending his department's swift response. "We take all threats seriously, and we will not tolerate any behavior that could put our students or staff at risk," he said.

Threatening Message and AirSoft Rifle

The message, posted on Tuesday, featured a photo of an AirSoft rifle with the caption "don't pu tomorrow." Police subsequently located the student and found him in possession of the AirSoft rifle depicted in the photo.

Charges and School Response

The student has been charged with False Public Alarm and Possession of an Imitation Firearm. He will not be attending Howell High School for the immediate future, according to Principal Jeremy Braverman.

"We understand that this incident may have caused concern among our students and families," Braverman said. "We want to assure everyone that there is no credible threat to the school, and we are working closely with law enforcement to ensure the safety of our community."

Increased Police Presence

As a precautionary measure, there will be an increased police presence at the school. "We want to make sure that our students and staff feel safe and secure," Kudrick said.

Michigan School Shooting

The incident in New Jersey occurred just hours after a tragic school shooting in Oxford, Michigan, where three students were killed and eight others injured. The victims have been identified as Tate Myre, Hana St. Juliana, and Madisyn Baldwin.

Overwhelming Sadness

Oakland County Sheriff Michael Bouchard expressed overwhelming sadness over the Michigan shooting and the impact on the community. "This is a senseless tragedy that has devastated our community," he said. "Our hearts go out to the victims and their families."

Call for Vigilance

In light of these recent events, law enforcement officials are urging the public to remain vigilant and report any suspicious activity. "If you see something, say something," Kudrick said. "We need the help of our community to keep our schools and our children safe."
