Gabby Petito's Death Ruled Strangulation, Autopsy Reveals

Teton County, WY - The Teton County Coroner, Dr. Brent Blue, has determined that Gabby Petito's cause of death was strangulation. The announcement comes after an autopsy was conducted on her remains, which were found at a campground in the Bridger-Teton National Forest on September 19.

"The cause of death is strangulation," Dr. Blue stated. "The manner of death is homicide."

The autopsy results also revealed that Petito's remains had likely been outside for three to four weeks before they were discovered. However, the exact time of her death could not be determined.

"We were not able to determine the exact time of death," Dr. Blue explained. "We can only determine the cause and manner of death."
