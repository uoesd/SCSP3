Twin Blasts Rock Kabul Airport, Killing Dozens

DW – 08 – Twin explosions outside Kabul's Hamid Karzai International Airport on Thursday killed dozens of Afghan civilians and at least 13 US soldiers, marking one of the deadliest days for American forces in Afghanistan in over a decade.

US Casualties Confirmed

General Kenneth McKenzie, Commander of US Central Command, confirmed the grim toll, stating that 13 US service members had been killed and 15 injured. "We are heartbroken by the loss of our service members," McKenzie said.

Biden Vows Revenge, Continues Evacuations

President Joe Biden condemned the attack as "heinous" and vowed to continue evacuations from Afghanistan. "We will not be deterred by terrorists. We will not let them stop our mission," Biden said. He also promised to avenge the fallen soldiers, saying, "We will hunt down the perpetrators and make them pay."

Intelligence Warnings Ignored

Intelligence warnings had indicated a terror threat from the Islamic State (ISIS) in the days leading up to the attack. However, it remains unclear why the warnings were not acted upon more effectively.

Explosions Target Airport Gates

The explosions occurred outside the airport's Abbey Gate and the nearby Baron Hotel, where many people were gathered to seek evacuation flights.

Taliban Condemns Attack

The Taliban, which recently took control of Afghanistan, condemned the attack and cited the presence of foreign forces as a cause for such incidents. "We strongly condemn the terrorist attack on Kabul airport," Taliban spokesman Zabihullah Mujahid said.

Casualty Toll Rises

The Emergency NGO reported that 60 injured individuals arrived at their Kabul Surgical Center, with six dead on arrival. The organization warned that the death toll could rise as more casualties are brought in.

International Condemnation

German Chancellor Angela Merkel and Foreign Minister Heiko Maas condemned the attack and expressed condolences to the victims. "This is a cowardly and despicable act that targets innocent people," Merkel said.

German Defense Minister Annegret Kramp-Karrenbauer announced that all German troops had left Afghanistan. "Our mission in Afghanistan is over," she said.

UK Prime Minister Boris Johnson called the attack "barbaric" and said it would not deter the UK's commitment to Afghanistan.

French President Emmanuel Macron condemned the "cowardly" attack and expressed solidarity with the victims.

UN Calls for Emergency Meeting

UN Secretary General Antonio Guterres called for an emergency Security Council meeting in response to the attack. "This is a despicable act of terrorism," Guterres said.

Eyewitness Accounts

Ali Latifi, a journalist who witnessed the attack, described the scene as "chaotic." "There was smoke and dust everywhere. People were running and screaming," he said.

Abdul Matin Amiri, a Kabul resident who was near the airport, said he saw "bodies lying on the ground." "It was a terrible sight," he said.

Ongoing Evacuations

Despite the attack, US and allied forces are continuing to evacuate people from Afghanistan. However, the security situation remains volatile, and further attacks are possible.
