Race and the Capitol Riot: A Tale of Two Responses

The January 6th Capitol riot has sparked a national debate about the role of race in law enforcement's response to protests. Critics argue that the Capitol Police's handling of the predominantly white mob that stormed the Capitol was markedly different from the way law enforcement has treated Black Lives Matter protests in recent years. This disparity has raised questions about systemic racism within law enforcement and American society at large.

Mark Anthony Neal: 'If the Protesters Were Black...'

Mark Anthony Neal, an African American studies professor at Duke University, believes that the Capitol riot would have been met with a much more forceful response if the protesters had been from Black Lives Matter. "If the protesters were Black, we would have seen a much more aggressive response from law enforcement," Neal said. "There would have been tear gas, rubber bullets, and arrests."

Criticism of Capitol Police Preparedness and Leadership

The Capitol Police have been widely criticized for their lack of preparedness and leadership during the riot. Former Chief Steven Sund resigned in the wake of the attack, and there have been calls for an independent commission to investigate the incident.

"The Capitol Police were not prepared for this," said Jeremy Pressman, an associate professor of political science and director of Middle East studies at the University of Connecticut. "They were outnumbered and outmaneuvered by the rioters."

Calls for an Independent Commission

House Speaker Nancy Pelosi has announced the creation of an independent commission to investigate the Capitol riot. The commission will be tasked with examining the events leading up to the attack, the law enforcement response, and the security failures that allowed the rioters to breach the Capitol.

Comparison of Law Enforcement Responses

The law enforcement response to the Capitol riot stands in stark contrast to the way law enforcement has treated Black Lives Matter protests in recent years. During the George Floyd protests, law enforcement often clashed with demonstrators despite the fact that the protests were largely peaceful.

"The difference in the law enforcement response to the Capitol riot and the Black Lives Matter protests is striking," said Brad Garrett, an ABC News contributor and retired FBI agent. "In the case of the Capitol riot, law enforcement was slow to respond and appeared to be overwhelmed by the situation. In contrast, law enforcement has often been quick to use force against Black Lives Matter protesters, even when the protests are peaceful."

A Failure of Law Enforcement

The Capitol riot was a clear failure of law enforcement. The Capitol Police were poorly prepared and lacked the leadership necessary to effectively respond to the threat. This failure has raised serious questions about the ability of law enforcement to protect the public from domestic terrorism.

"The Capitol riot was a wake-up call for law enforcement," said President Joe Biden. "We need to do a better job of training our officers and preparing them for the threats we face today."

Recent History of Disproportionate Police Responses

The Capitol riot is not an isolated incident. Recent history is replete with examples of law enforcement responding disproportionately to protests based on the racial composition of the demonstrators.

One example is the Michael Brown protests in Ferguson, Missouri. In 2014, Brown, an unarmed black teenager, was shot and killed by a white police officer. The shooting sparked widespread protests, which were met with a heavy-handed response from law enforcement.

"The Ferguson protests were a clear example of how law enforcement can use excessive force against black protesters," said Neal. "The police used tear gas, rubber bullets, and armored vehicles to disperse the protesters, even though the protests were largely peaceful."

Reconsidering Racism in Law Enforcement and Society

The Capitol riot has prompted a national conversation about racism within law enforcement and American society at large. Many people are now questioning whether law enforcement is biased against black people and other minorities.

"The Capitol riot has forced us to confront the issue of racism in law enforcement," said Pressman. "We need to have a serious discussion about how we can address this problem and ensure that all Americans are treated fairly by law enforcement."

Conclusion

The Capitol riot was a dark day for American democracy. It was also a day that exposed the deep racial divide that exists in our country.
