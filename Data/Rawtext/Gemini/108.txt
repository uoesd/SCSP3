CNN chief Jeff Zucker informed Cuomo of the decision in a memo to staff, emphasizing the complexity of such decisions. "This is not a decision we made lightly," Zucker wrote. "We respect Chris's talent and his work."

Cuomo expressed disappointment in a Twitter statement but was proud of his team's work. "This is not how I wanted my time at CNN to end but I have always been loyal to CNN and its viewers," he wrote.

The investigation into Cuomo's conduct will continue "as appropriate," according to CNN. Cuomo had previously sought information on accusers and assisted in crafting responses to allegations.

CNN had been under scrutiny for months over Cuomo's conduct even before his firing. Some critics accused the network of double standards for suspending Chris Cuomo but not his brother, who resigned in August amid multiple harassment allegations.

The firing leaves a notable gap in CNN's prime-time lineup. Cuomo's 9 p.m. ET show was one of the network's most popular.

Allegations resurfaced of Cuomo groping a female colleague 16 years ago, to which he has previously apologized.
