Vice President Harris Announces Collaborative Initiatives with France on Space and Cybersecurity

Washington, D.C. - Vice President Kamala Harris, during her meeting with French President Emmanuel Macron, announced a series of collaborative initiatives between the United States and France on space and cybersecurity. These initiatives underscore the Biden-Harris Administration's commitment to working closely with allies to address 21st-century challenges.

Space Cooperation

The United States and France will enhance their cooperation in the civil, commercial, and national security sectors of space. The two countries will work together to address global issues such as the climate crisis, space exploration, STEM education, space environment sustainability, and the space economy.

A key component of this cooperation is the establishment of a comprehensive dialogue on space. This dialogue will involve participation from various U.S. and French departments and agencies, ensuring a coordinated approach to space-related issues.

The United States has also committed to joining the Space Climate Observatory (SCO), a French-led initiative that aims to make space data accessible for climate crisis mitigation and response. This collaboration will provide valuable insights into the impact of human activities on the space environment and contribute to global efforts to address climate change.

Cybersecurity Cooperation

Vice President Harris reaffirmed the United States' commitment to working with allies on cybersecurity and upholding global norms in cyberspace. She announced U.S. support for the Paris Call for Trust and Security in Cyberspace, a French-led initiative that aims to advance cybersecurity globally.

The Biden-Harris Administration has made international cybersecurity engagement a priority. The United States will continue to work with partners through various international organizations and initiatives, including actions against cybercrime and updates to NATO cyber policy.

Strengthening the Alliance

These initiatives on space and cybersecurity demonstrate the strong and enduring partnership between the United States and France. They reflect the shared commitment of both countries to addressing global challenges and promoting international cooperation.

The United States and France have a long history of collaboration in space and cybersecurity. These new initiatives will build on this foundation and further strengthen the alliance between the two countries.

The United States will continue to work with France and other allies to address the evolving challenges of space and cybersecurity. These initiatives are a testament to the Biden-Harris Administration's commitment to international cooperation and the advancement of global security and prosperity.
