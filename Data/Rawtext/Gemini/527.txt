FEMA Assistance Deadline Extended for Union County Hurricane Ida Survivors

Union County, NJ â€“ Union County residents affected by Hurricane Ida now have until January 5, 2022, to apply for individual assistance from the Federal Emergency Management Agency (FEMA).

"This extension is critical to ensuring that our residents have ample time to access the federal assistance they need to recover from the devastating impacts of Hurricane Ida," said Commissioner Board Chairman Alexander Mirabella.

The FEMA Disaster Recovery Center in Union County will remain open indefinitely to provide in-person assistance to residents. The center is located at the O'Donnell Dempsey Senior Community Center in Elizabeth, NJ. Operating hours are Monday through Friday, 8:00 a.m. to 6:00 p.m., and Saturdays, 8:00 a.m. to 5:00 p.m.

Federal disaster assistance may include financial aid for rent, essential home repairs, and other needs not covered by insurance. Residents can apply for assistance online at DisasterAssistance.gov, via the FEMA app, or by calling the FEMA Helpline at 1-800-621-FEMA (3362).

"We encourage all eligible residents to apply for FEMA assistance as soon as possible," said Mirabella. "This assistance can make a significant difference in helping families and individuals rebuild their lives after this storm."

In addition to FEMA assistance, Union County is also providing free legal services to residents facing legal issues related to Hurricane Ida. Legal assistance covers benefits, insurance claims, document replacement, and counseling on financial issues.

Union County is among 12 NJ counties designated for FEMA disaster relief following the impacts of Hurricane Ida. The other counties include Bergen, Essex, Gloucester, Hudson, Hunterdon, Middlesex, Monmouth, Morris, Passaic, Somerset, and Warren.
