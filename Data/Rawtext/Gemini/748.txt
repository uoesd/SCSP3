Hollywood Reels from Tragedy: Brandon Lee's Family Demands Ban on Real Guns After 'Rust' Shooting

The tragic death of cinematographer Halyna Hutchins on the set of the Western film "Rust" has reignited a long-standing debate about the use of real guns on film sets. Brandon Lee's family, who lost their son to a similar tragedy in 1993, has expressed profound sadness and frustration over the incident.

"There is no reason to have live ammunition on a movie set," said Lee. "Modern special effects can replicate the look and sound of gunfire without putting anyone at risk."
