Tiger Woods Returns Home After Hospital Stay Following Car Accident

Tiger Woods has returned home to South Florida after spending 21 days in the hospital following a car accident in California.

The accident occurred on February 23rd on the border of Rancho Palos Verdes and Rolling Hills Estates, Calif. Woods suffered comminuted fractures to the tibia and fibula bones in his right leg, as well as foot and ankle injuries.

After initial treatment at Harbor-UCLA Medical Center, Woods was moved to Cedars-Sinai Medical Center in Los Angeles for successful follow-up procedures.

In a statement released on his social media accounts, Woods thanked the medical personnel involved in his care. "I am so grateful for the outpouring of support and encouragement that I have received over the past few weeks," Woods said. "Thank you to the incredible surgeons, doctors, nurses and staff at Harbor-UCLA Medical Center and Cedars-Sinai Medical Center. Your expertise, attention and care have been remarkable, and I am eternally grateful."

Woods has not provided a public timeline for his recovery or information about his future in competitive golf. However, he expressed his determination to get stronger every day. "I will continue to focus on my recovery and rehabilitation," Woods said. "Thank you for your patience and support."

The sports world, including PGA Tour pros, showed their support for Woods by wearing his iconic "Sunday Red" during the final round of the Genesis Invitational at Riviera Country Club.

Woods' statement was met with an outpouring of support from fans and fellow athletes. Many expressed their admiration for his resilience and wished him a speedy recovery.

Woods' return home marks a significant milestone in his recovery. He will continue to receive medical care and rehabilitation as he works to regain his strength and mobility.
