Louisiana Reels from Ida's Wrath: Power Outages, Disparity, and Ongoing Struggles

LaPLACE, La. â€“ Nine days after Hurricane Ida ravaged Louisiana, hundreds of thousands of residents remain without power, grappling with a disparity in restoration efforts that has left some communities feeling abandoned.

Power Disparity Fuels Frustration

"It's like we've been forgotten," said Kim Bass, a resident of St. John the Baptist Parish, where power outages have persisted for days. "New Orleans has power, but we're still in the dark."

The disparity in power restoration has drawn criticism from state officials. "This is unacceptable," said State Rep. Tanner Magee (House's second-ranking Republican). "Our communities deserve better."

Entergy Louisiana, the state's largest utility, has been working to restore power, but some areas may not have electricity until the end of September. "We're making progress, but the damage is extensive," said Phillip May, Entergy Louisiana President and CEO.

Nursing Home Deaths Prompt License Revocations

The state has revoked the licenses of seven nursing homes after seven residents died in post-Ida conditions. The deaths have raised concerns about the care provided to vulnerable residents during the storm.

Hospitals Struggle to Reopen

Ochsner Health's two hospitals in the hardest-hit parishes are operating emergency rooms but are not fully open. "We're doing everything we can to get our hospitals back up and running," said Warner Thomas, President and CEO of Ochsner Health. "But the damage is significant."

Carnival Glory Docks in New Orleans

The Carnival Glory cruise ship has docked in New Orleans to house first responders and provide medical assistance. The ship is equipped with hospital beds, operating rooms, and a pharmacy.

Ongoing Challenges

Louisiana continues to struggle with fuel shortages, water service intermittency, and uninhabitable homes due to Ida. About 3,200 people are in mass shelters, while 25,000 are in FEMA hotel rooms for transitional sheltering.

"We're still in crisis mode," said Michael Lathers, a resident of LaPlace. "We need help."

FEMA Assistance

FEMA has approved 159,000 household applications for disaster assistance. The agency is providing financial assistance, housing, and other support to affected residents.

Death Toll Rises

The death toll from Ida in Louisiana has risen to 15, with additional fatalities due to injury and lack of oxygen during the power outage.

Long Road Ahead

Louisiana faces a long and difficult road to recovery from Hurricane Ida. The state is working to restore power, provide assistance to those in need, and rebuild damaged infrastructure.

"We're going to get through this together," said Governor John Bel Edwards. "But it's going to take time and a lot of hard work."
